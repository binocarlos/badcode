**Scene (planned):** The two sit together by the tent and the barrel fire in the
ruined car park, sharing what little there is. Quiet, near-wordless. This is the
one panel where contained warmth is *earned* — the human heat the whole comic
withheld. Bob's grace is in the small offered gesture and the eyes, not a grand
expression.

**Narration:** "He has every reason to gloat. He doesn't. The man who was certain that poverty was deserved is living it now — and the only one who treats him as human is the one he sneered at."
