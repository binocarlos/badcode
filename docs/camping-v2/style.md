# Camping (v2) — style notes (thin)

The **global** BadCode house style lives in the `badcode-art-direction` skill
(35mm documentary look, muted cool-neutral palette, observational framing,
environment-as-argument, restraint vs. reveal). **Read that first.** This file is
only the Camping-specific specializations it can't know — read it *alongside* the
skill, never as an override.

## Recurring locations (keep consistent across panels)

- **The Shard / City interior** (p02–p05) — cold glass, flat fluorescent + grey
  daylight, self-satisfied, no warmth.
- **The Waitrose car park** (p06–p09, and ruined in p15–p20) — wet tarmac, low
  wall, trolley bay. The same place in its before (ordinary, overcast) and after
  (frost, tents, wreckage, a burning barrel). Class-coded and recognisable.
- **The Gwydir Forest river clearing** (p11–p14) — executive dome tents, fog, a
  small ceremony fire; Welsh, damp, late-70s film-negative texture on exteriors.

## Where warmth is allowed (and only here)

Warmth is an argument in this comic, never decoration:

1. The **wank tank's gold headlights** through Welsh fog (p10) — opulence + menace.
2. The **ceremony fire** (p11–p12) — contained, slightly sickly.
3. The **barrel fire in the ruins** (p18–p19) — the one *earned* human warmth, the
   heat the whole comic withheld, shared between the two men at the end.

Everywhere else: muted, cool, unforgiving.

## Motifs to hold

- **The tent** is one object in every appearance (see `characters/tent.md`).
- **The POV-swap rhyme:** p09 (Bob's tent-out shot) and p15 (Tarquin's) must share
  the exact door-flap framing and low horizon.
- **The blink** (p13 reflection, p15 five-year jump) is the scroll device — the
  stills hold the "after" state; the close/reopen is the comic effect in Phase 4.
- **Recognisability across the jump:** ruined-Tarquin (p16–p20) is the same bone
  structure as Shard-Tarquin, five years wrecked.
