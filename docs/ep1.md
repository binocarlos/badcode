# EP1 — the teaser

**EP1** is exactly that: a short, three-track teaser — the first thing the world sees from BadCode.
The name is deliberately plain (`EP1`, no clever title), because the point isn't to overclaim, it's
to *hook*. EP1 sits beneath the overarching project, **[GitPush Origin Master](./gitpush-origin-master.md)** —
the large timeline everything else hangs from.

How we frame it publicly: **BadCode — EP1, from _GitPush Origin Master_ (coming soon).**

Each EP1 story ships as **two** things for now:

1. **A comic** — AI-generated imagery, presented as a scroll-driven story on this site.
2. **A music track** — drum & bass.

> The per-story **AI agent** (a topic specialist in the fictional "BadCode government") is **not**
> part of EP1. It comes later and is built in a separate project. We launch with comic + track only.

## Tracklist

### 1. Camping
Wealth inequality, homelessness, and the coming automation of white-collar work. Tarquin, a smug
tech bro, judges Bob, a homeless man — then a Christmas-Carol-style "Ghost of Economic Future" shows
him Bob's past and, worse, a near future where Tarquin's own world has collapsed (boarded-up
Waitrose, 99% unemployment) and it's Bob who shows him kindness. The point: the people doing
everything "right" are the least prepared for what's coming.

### 2. Karen Will Lead the Revolution
The "Karen" stereotype, flipped into an asset. Karen is automated out of a 30-year job (severance: a
£100 voucher), gets trapped in AI benefits bots, and starts escalating — "I want to speak to your
manager" — all the way up the chain to the Prime Minister. A century later, a classroom learns about
the Great Karen Revolution that won universal basic income and democratic ownership of automated
systems. Nobody is better equipped to refuse "no" than Karen.

### 3. Emperor's New Coin
The fiction at the heart of the economy: money itself. A nod to *The Emperor's New Clothes* —
everyone agrees to see a value that was conjured from nothing. The track tears through how money is
actually made and unmade — fiat, quantitative easing, fractional reserves, "there is no magic money
tree" said to a nurse while trillions appear for the banks — and how that quiet debasement inflates
the assets the wealthy already own. The point: the scarcity of *money* is a story we're told, not a
law of physics.

*Still in active development.* The strongest companion concept is a token that tracks the M2 money
supply against fixed-price NFTs, making asset inflation visible in real time.

## In development (not on EP1)

Same world, not on the teaser — held in the pool until they earn a slot. **Fire Sale** (democracy
auctioned off through donations and lobbying) and **Out of the Jungle** (a post-scarcity abundance
fable) were earlier EP1 candidates and now sit here; we're simply not pushing them yet. Also in the
pool: **Magic Money Tree** (QE as a fable), **We Can Afford What We Can Create** (MMT / Keynes),
**Billionaire Coin** (gamifying "get the rich to pay tax"), **Bank Robbery**, and **Fork and Fold**
(chemical vs. digital evolution). These live in [`ideas/`](../ideas) as we develop them.
