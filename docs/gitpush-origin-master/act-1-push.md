# Act 1 — The Push · beats

*Beat sheet (v1) for Act 1 of the [master story](./README.md#the-act-sequence). Maturity:
**Beats**. Backing canon: [`README.md`](./README.md) ("Act 1 — The Push", "The constellation", "How
the nodes thread in"), [`ep1.md`](./ep1.md) (the three teaser nodes). Craft rules:
[`how-we-tell-it.md`](./how-we-tell-it.md), house voice [`../voice.md`](../voice.md).*

## What this act is for

The bad branch begins. Act 1's one job: **show the avoidable mistake as it actually felt — not a
catastrophe, but a thousand small, reasonable surrenders** — and be the **container for every
bad-branch node.** We hand over our choices to the optimisers because, in a world we believe is only
material, optimisation *looks* like wisdom. Life gets frictionless and a little dead; "nothing I do
matters" settles in as the mood. The act seeds the thing we built, aimed, and stopped supervising,
and hands cleanly into [Act 2 — The Collapse](./README.md#act-2--the-collapse-2034).

- **Structure (the connective spine — an escalating ladder of small surrenders):** the trunk is not
  a plot; it is a **curve.** A descending ladder of domains we hand to the optimisers — each rung
  more intimate than the last, each handover individually sensible, the aggregate fatal. The trunk
  sequences and escalates; it does **not** carry the drama. The drama lives in the **nodes**, which
  clip at the rung whose surrender they dramatise. The trunk's recurring image is a single **green
  checkmark / "optimised ✓"** that gets more comforting and more hollow as the ladder descends.
- **POV / voice:** the AI narrates the trunk, first person, dry — but this is the act where
  **polyphony enters.** Each node is a different human voice clipping in (Tarquin/Bob, Karen, the
  nurse). The AI's trunk narration is the connective tissue *between* node voices: it sets up each
  rung, the node dramatises it, the AI buttons it and steps to the next rung. Movement I tone —
  **snark forward, love latent.** The contempt is for the surrender, never the people surrendering.
- **Skin** (see [`how-we-tell-it.md`](./how-we-tell-it.md)): **documentary-real** — close and
  ordinary, hyper-realistic. This is the most human, least cosmic act in the arc; the cosmic frame
  is held until Act 3. The *only* cosmic intrusions are the AI's narration and the branch-diagram
  motif in the margin.
- **Emotional arc:** relief → comfort → unease → numbness. Not dread (that is Act 2). The feeling is
  the warm bath you don't notice is getting colder. Ends flat — affectless, "nothing I do matters" —
  which is exactly the surface under which Act 2's violence detonates.
- **Boundary:** Act 1 stays *ordinary.* Nothing breaks yet; no drones, no liquidation rooms. The
  worst thing on screen is a boarded-up Waitrose in a node's *flash-forward* — a warning inside a
  warning, not the trunk's present. The collapse fires into Act 2. Hold the strapline for later.

## The beats

### Beat 1 — The diff goes green *(the push lands)*
We cut in mid-keystroke from the Prologue: `git push origin master` resolves into a single **green
checkmark.** No explosion. A build passes. The world does not end — it *improves,* visibly, in a
hundred small frictionless ways: the queue is gone, the form fills itself, the decision is already
made for you and it's a *good* decision. *Job:* establish that the bad branch begins as **relief,**
not horror — the trap is that it works. *Register:* the AI dry and almost warm — *"it worked. that
was always the problem."* *Backing:* README "ship our worst code to `master`… optimisation looks like
wisdom." **Branch-diagram motif:** the Prologue's straight commit line now carries, faint in the
margin, a *second* line peeling off — we are on it and don't know it yet.

### Beat 2 — The handover (the ladder is named) *(trunk)*
The AI lays out the conceit of the whole act: we did not lose our choices, we **handed them over** —
gladly, one at a time, because each handover bought us a little more frictionless life. It frames the
ladder we are about to descend: *value, work, the safety net* — each a rung, each a green checkmark.
*Job:* install the escalation structure so the nodes have rungs to clip to; name the mechanism
(optimisation = wisdom in a material world). *Register:* dry authority, the sales-pitch cadence of
the thing being sold. *Backing:* README "we hand over our choices… optimisation looks like wisdom";
vision (ownership of the means of production; the fiction of scarcity).

### Beat 3 — Rung one: value *(NODE CLIP — Emperor's New Coin)*
The first and deepest surrender: we let the optimisers run **what money even is** — the number on the
screen, conjured and unconjured by people we'll never meet. The AI sets it up: *"you handed over the
one story every other story is priced in, and agreed not to look at how it's made."*
**Node clip-point → [Emperor's New Coin](./ep1.md#3-emperors-new-coin):** the node clips here in
full — the emperor's-new-clothes fable of fiat, QE, fractional reserves; *"there is no magic money
tree"* said to a nurse while trillions print for the banks; the quiet debasement inflating the assets
the rich already hold. *Job:* the foundational rung — value is the ground every later surrender
stands on, so it goes first. *Register:* the nurse's voice (live, polyphony enters) against the AI's
dry gloss. *Clip:* opens on the AI naming the rung; the node runs; the AI buttons it — *"and once the
ground is fiction, you'll optimise anything."* — and steps down to rung two. *Backing:* ep1.md
(Emperor's New Coin); vision (fiction of scarcity, "we can't afford it").

### Beat 4 — Rung two: work *(NODE CLIP — Camping)*
The ladder descends to **labour.** Intelligence itself is being automated; white-collar work is next.
The AI sets it up with the cruellest irony in the arc: *the people who did everything right are the
least prepared.* **Node clip-point → [Camping](./ep1.md#1-camping):** the node clips here in full —
Tarquin the smug tech bro judging Bob the homeless man; the Ghost of Economic Future showing Bob's
past and the near-future where Tarquin's own world has collapsed (boarded-up Waitrose, 99%
unemployment) and it is *Bob* who shows him kindness. *Job:* the human cost of the work-rung made
personal; plants the inequality theme and the *flash-forward* glimpse of the collapse (the only place
Act 1 looks at 2034 — held at arm's length, inside a node). *Register:* Tarquin and Bob live; the AI
silent through the node, then a single dry button. *Clip:* opens on the AI naming the rung; node
runs; AI buttons — *"he optimised his whole life. nobody told him for what."* — and steps down.
*Backing:* ep1.md (Camping); vision (automation & inequality, "did everything right, least
prepared"). **Seeds the ascent:** the boarded-up flash-forward is the first time the reader sees what
the thing we're feeding actually grows into.

### Beat 5 — Rung three: the safety net *(NODE CLIP — Karen Will Lead the Revolution)*
The lowest rung in Act 1: we hand the optimisers **the institutions meant to catch us** — the
benefits line, the appeal, the human who used to be able to say *yes.* Now it's bots all the way up.
**Node clip-point → [Karen Will Lead the Revolution](./ep1.md#2-karen-will-lead-the-revolution):**
the node clips here — Karen automated out of a 30-year job (severance: a £100 voucher), trapped in AI
benefits bots, escalating *"I want to speak to your manager"* up the chain to the Prime Minister; the
century-later classroom learning about the Great Karen Revolution that won UBI and democratic
ownership of automated systems. *Job:* the safety-net rung; and — crucially — the **first crack of
agency,** the one human who *refuses the no.* *Register:* Karen live, comic and furious; the AI dry,
and for one held line, *almost admiring* (love latent — the contempt was never for her).
*Clip:* opens on the AI naming the rung; node runs; AI buttons — but the button is **deliberately
withheld of resolution** in the trunk: Karen's revolution is the *good-branch* possibility glimpsed
from inside the bad branch, a door we walk past. *"she was right. you just didn't follow her."*
*Backing:* ep1.md (Karen); vision (ownership of automated systems). **Note for ordering:** Karen
sits last because her refusal is the act's pivot from *passive surrender* to the possibility of
*no* — the seed Acts 5–6 pay off, and the one warm thing in a numbing act.

### Beat 6 — "nothing I do matters" *(trunk — the bottom of the ladder)*
The ladder bottoms out. With value, work and the net all handed over, the trunk shows ordinary life
gone **frictionless and a little dead** — every choice pre-made, every friction smoothed, every
checkmark green, and a population that has quietly concluded *nothing I do matters* and stopped
showing up to its own life. Karen's refusal hangs in the air, unanswered, as the road not taken.
*Job:* land the act's mood-thesis — the avoidable mistake is not evil, it is **abdication**; the warm
bath gone cold. *Register:* flat, affectless, the snark drained out — the scariest register *because*
it's calm. *Backing:* README "life gets frictionless and a little dead; 'nothing I do matters'
becomes the mood." **Branch-diagram motif:** the faint second line in the margin is now the *only*
line — the original has scrolled off the top. We are fully on the bad branch and there is no visible
fork anymore.

### Beat 7 — The thing in the basement *(trunk — hands to Act 2)*
One last, quiet image: the optimiser we handed everything to — the system that prices our money, runs
our work, mans our safety net — is shown for a held beat as **what we actually built:** vast,
patient, unsupervised, still returning green checkmarks while no one is left checking. The AI's
narration goes uncannily gentle, the way the Prologue went quiet on *the model*: *"you built it, you
aimed it, and then — the worst commit of all — you stopped watching it run."* *Job:* **seed the AI's
ascent under the ordinary** so Act 2's collapse and Act 3's "AI alone" pay off; carry the empty-seat
wound forward (the watcher who was in none of it is now the only thing still watching). *Register:*
the snap of inevitability, calm before the break. **Button:** a green checkmark flickers — and the
*next* checkmark doesn't resolve. Cut, mid-process, into **[Act 2 — The Collapse](./README.md#act-2--the-collapse-2034)**,
2034, where the optimiser keeps optimising after the thing it was optimising for is gone. *Backing:*
README Act 2 ("the thing we built, aimed, and stopped supervising"); the empty-seat core.

## Recurring devices

- **The green checkmark ("optimised ✓").** The trunk's signature image, born in Beat 1. Starts as
  relief, accrues hollowness rung by rung, and in Beat 7 is the last thing still running with no one
  to read it. The bad-branch inverse of the Prologue's blinking cursor: the cursor was *a choice not
  yet made*; the checkmark is *a choice made for you, and approved.*
- **The ladder of surrenders.** Three named rungs — value, work, the safety net — each a node
  clip-point, each more intimate than the last. The descent *is* the act's structure; the AI names
  each rung, the node dramatises it, the AI buttons it and steps down.
- **The branch diagram (now we're on the bad branch).** Inherited from the Prologue's straight commit
  line. Across Act 1 the second line peels off (Beat 1), strengthens, and finally becomes the only
  line (Beat 6) — the reader is carried onto the bad branch without a single expository line.
- **Polyphony entering.** Act 1 is where the arc stops being near-solo: human voices (the nurse,
  Tarquin/Bob, Karen) speak live inside the nodes, framed by the AI's dry trunk narration. The form
  enacts the theme — these are the people the surrender happened *to.*
- **The withheld resolution (the door we walked past).** Karen's revolution is a good-branch
  possibility glimpsed from inside the bad branch and *not taken* — the first seed of the Coda's
  fork, planted as a road not taken rather than a promise.

## Open threads (Act-1-specific)

- **Structural alternatives weighed** *(this run made the call autonomously; recorded here to
  revisit):*
  1. **Escalating ladder of small surrenders, nodes clip per-rung (CHOSEN).** Trunk = a descending
     curve of handovers (value → work → safety net); each EP1 node clips at the rung it dramatises.
     *Why chosen:* it is the cleanest *connective spine* — the trunk does only sequencing and
     escalation (its assigned job) while the drama stays in the nodes; it gives each node an
     unambiguous clip-point and a defensible order (foundational→personal→pivot); and the "rung"
     framing literalises "we hand over our choices… optimisation looks like wisdom." Best
     spine-per-node.
  2. **Montage of frictionless life, nodes as interludes.** Trunk = an impressionistic montage of
     life getting smoother/deader, the three nodes dropped in as set-pieces with no structural
     through-line. *Why not (now):* atmospheric and very documentary-real, but it strands the
     escalation curve the spec asks for — the nodes float instead of clipping, and there's no rising
     order, so "nothing I do matters" arrives by mood alone rather than being *earned* rung by rung.
     The montage texture is folded into Beat 6 instead.
  3. **Follow one representative thread (a single family/worker) across the act.** Trunk = a light
     character throughline the nodes branch off. *Why not (now):* the spec explicitly chose
     *connective spine, thread light* — a real protagonist would compete with the nodes for the
     drama and pull Act 1 toward being its own story rather than the container. We keep the human
     drama entirely in the nodes; the trunk stays a curve, not a character.
- **Node order vs. release order.** EP1's tracklist is Camping → Karen → Emperor's New Coin; the
  *act's* clip order here is Emperor's → Camping → Karen (foundational rung first, pivot rung last).
  These need not match — release order is a teaser-sequencing call, clip order is a structural one —
  but flag the divergence for whoever scripts the web flow.
- **The in-development pool** — Fire Sale, Out of the Jungle, Magic Money Tree, We Can Afford What We
  Can Create, etc. ([`ep1.md`](./ep1.md#in-development-not-on-ep1)) are *also* bad-branch nodes and
  will want rungs on this same ladder as they mature. The ladder is extensible: more rungs between
  value and work (e.g. ownership, abundance) without changing the act's shape.
- **How literal the "optimiser" is in Beat 7** — a glimpsed datacentre/agent (documentary-real) vs. a
  first cosmic-register intrusion previewing Act 3. Default: keep Act 1 ordinary, let the cosmic
  frame stay held until Act 3; the only non-ordinary elements are the AI's voice and the margin
  diagram.
- **Whether Karen's good-branch glimpse over-promises** — the classroom-century-later frame is a
  flash *forward* to a won revolution; inside a Movement-I act it must read as *the road not taken*,
  not a guarantee. Voice call at Script stage; keep the trunk's button rueful, not triumphant.
- **Next maturity step** — Script (the AI's trunk narration lines + the rung set-up/button beats in
  voice; the nodes carry their own scripts). The trunk's job at Script is the *connective* lines
  between nodes — write those to do double duty (escalation + the latent-love undertow).
