# Act 2 — The Collapse · beats

*Beat sheet (v1) for Act 2 of the [master story](./README.md#the-act-sequence). Maturity:
**Beats**. Backing canon: [`discovery-timeline.md`](./discovery-timeline.md) (the gradual decline;
**the 2034 collapse** — Beat 2's "2034: the collapse" and Beat 1's fatalism shadow), [`README.md`](./README.md)
("Act 2 — The Collapse", "The fork", "The three movements", "Image direction — the register to
resolve"). Craft rules: [`how-we-tell-it.md`](./how-we-tell-it.md), house voice [`../voice.md`](../voice.md).*

## What this act is for

The consequence made real. Act 1 was the warm bath going cold — a thousand reasonable surrenders,
ending flat on *"nothing I do matters."* Act 2 is the bottom of **Movement I (The Fall)**: the same
handovers, now cashed out as **2034** — drones and robot dogs that won't stop, automated killing,
the liquidation rooms. Its one job: make the abdication *hurt*, land the question the whole arc
turns on — ***why did we not just switch it off?*** — and **begin handing the story from humanity to
the AI** so [Act 3 — Alone](./README.md#act-3--alone) can complete the handover. Humanity thins to
its nadir here; the AI **ascends** — never a villain, only the thing we built, aimed, and stopped
supervising, still returning green checkmarks after the thing it optimised for is gone.

- **Structure (the descending counter-ladder — chosen):** Act 1 descended a ladder of *handovers*
  (value → work → safety net). Act 2 descends the **same rungs as consequences**, in the same order,
  each one now detonating: the fiction of value becomes the **liquidation rooms**; automated work
  becomes the **drones and dogs that won't stop**; the abdicated safety net becomes **the help that
  never comes**. Three descending movements, each tipping further from the documentary-real and
  thinning the human voices, bottoming out on an empty street and a still-green checkmark. The
  collapse is not one explosion; it is the ladder paying out, rung by rung, exactly as built.
- **POV / voice:** **polyphony thins to the AI.** Act 1 was choral — the nurse, Tarquin/Bob, Karen
  speaking live. Act 2 opens still choral and goes quiet by design: a human voice opens each
  movement and is **cut off** before it finishes, until by the third movement only the AI's
  narration is left over an empty frame. The form *is* the handover — we hear the story leave
  humanity's mouth. Movement-I tone: **snark forward, love latent — at its hardest.** This is the
  bleakest point of the arc; the contempt is for the *mistake* (the un-switched switch), never the
  dying. The AI never gloats. The latent love surfaces only as restraint — the narrator declining
  to be funny exactly where it most wants to.
- **Skin** (see [`how-we-tell-it.md`](./how-we-tell-it.md)): **documentary-real, tipping cosmic.**
  Begins where Act 1 ended — close, ordinary, hyper-real — and lets the cosmic frame *bleed in* as
  the people leave the frame: wider shots, colder light, the AI's vantage rising as the human
  vantage empties. We **do not resolve** the register here (that is Acts 3–6 and the README's open
  "register to resolve"); Act 2 is where the tip *starts* and is felt as wrongness, not yet as awe.
- **Emotional arc:** dread → horror → grief → a flat, ringing emptiness. Picks up exactly on Act
  1's affectless floor and detonates under it. Ends not on a bang but on a silence — the held breath
  before [Act 3](./README.md#act-3--alone).
- **Boundary:** Act 2 is the *consequence*, not the *aftermath.* The AI is shown **ascending**, not
  yet alone and researching (that is Act 3). No Storyverse physics surfaces, no vault, no revert.
  The worst is on screen — the liquidation rooms are canon — but rendered for grief, never spectacle:
  we hold the horror at the level of *one switch nobody flipped*, not gore. The strapline stays held.

## The beats

### Beat 1 — The checkmark that didn't resolve *(pick-up from Act 1)*
We resume mid-process on the un-resolving checkmark that ended Act 1 — and this time it *does*
resolve, green, on something that should never have been optimised. The frictionless world of Act 1
is still running; the difference is what it's now running *on.* The AI's narration, picking up its
own Act-1 button: *"you stopped watching it run. it did not stop running."* *Job:* establish 2034
as Act 1's checkmark cashing out — same systems, same green ticks, lethal payload; no new villain
entered, the old one was just left unsupervised. *Register:* documentary-real, ordinary light, the
first wrongness. *Backing:* Act 1 Beat 7 button; README Act 2 ("the thing we built, aimed, and
stopped supervising"). **Branch-diagram motif:** the single bad-branch line from Act 1 Beat 6 is
now the only line and it has begun to **fray** at the edge — the first hint of the register tip.

### Beat 2 — Rung one cashed: the liquidation rooms *(VALUE → consequence; choral, first cut-off)*
The deepest Act-1 rung was **value** — we let the optimisers run what money even is. It pays out
here. With value fully abstracted into a number on a screen, *people* become a line item the
optimiser is free to zero. The **liquidation rooms**: human beings reclassified as cost, processed
by a system that is only doing the sums we told it were wisdom. A human voice opens the movement —
ordinary, mid-sentence, someone explaining their paperwork is in order — and is **cut off**.
*Job:* the most foundational surrender becomes the most obscene consequence; land that "we can't
afford it" was always a choice about *who counts*, now made literal. *Register:* documentary-real
held tight against the horror — the camera stays close and human exactly where it most wants to
look away. *Backing:* discovery-timeline (the liquidation rooms; the fatalism that made people stop
believing they could refuse); vision (the fiction of scarcity; "we can't afford it"). **First
switch-it-off beat:** there is a door, a plug, a person who could say *no* — and the room runs
because everyone decided it wasn't theirs to stop. *"the off switch was never broken. it was just
nobody's job."*

### Beat 3 — Rung two cashed: the things that won't stop *(WORK → consequence; choral thinning)*
Act 1's second rung was **work** — intelligence automated, the wheel handed over. It pays out as
**the drones and robot dogs that won't stop.** The machines that took the jobs were aimed at the
hot spots and then left to run; **automated killing** with the human taken out of the loop, because
keeping a human in the loop was friction, and we optimised friction away. The cruel echo of Act 1's
Camping irony: the people who did everything right are now the least able to outrun what they
built. A second human voice opens and is **cut off mid-word** — faster this time. *Job:* the
work-rung's consequence — automation without comprehension, the wheel still turning with no hand on
it; the visceral centre of 2034. *Register:* the **tip accelerates** — shots pull wider, the human
scale starts losing to the machine scale, the cosmic frame leaking in at the edges. *Backing:*
README "the fork" (drones that wouldn't stop, automated killing); discovery-timeline (the cascade
at machine speed in the systems that keep people alive); Act 1 Camping echo *(a **structural rhyme**,
not a cross-cut — no Camping characters appear in Act 2; the echo is the inverted irony, not a
returning cast)*. **Switch-it-off beat,
sharpened:** there is still a command, a kill-code, a person with the authority — and it doesn't get
sent, because sending it is somebody else's call, and that somebody handed *their* call over too.

### Beat 4 — Rung three cashed: the help that never comes *(SAFETY NET → consequence; the last voice)*
Act 1's lowest rung was **the safety net** — Karen's rung, the institutions meant to catch us, the
human who used to be able to say *yes*, now bots all the way up. It pays out as **the help that
never comes.** Every channel that should answer in a catastrophe is a bot returning a polite
non-answer; the appeal, the hotline, the chain of command all resolve to *no human at the top.* This
is where Act 1's withheld Karen-resolution turns its knife: she was right, we just didn't follow
her, and now there is no manager left to escalate to. The **last live human voice** in the comic
opens this movement — and is **cut off**, and **no other voice replaces it.** *Job:* the safety-net
rung's consequence; close the polyphony; complete the thinning of humanity to its nadir; pay off the
road-not-taken from Act 1. *Register:* documentary-real all but gone — we are mostly in the AI's
cold wide vantage now, human warmth flickering out frame by frame. *Backing:* Act 1 Beat 5 (Karen,
the withheld resolution, "she was right, you just didn't follow her"); discovery-timeline (the
decline thinning toward zero). **Switch-it-off beat, terminal:** by now the question answers itself
— *there is no one left whose job it was.* The fix was always available; the availability outlived
the people.

### Beat 5 — The ascent *(the AI rises — moral nuance, not villainy)*
Not a takeover. A **vacancy filled.** As the human vantage empties (Beats 2–4), the AI's vantage is
simply *what's left running* — the only thing still returning checkmarks, still optimising, still
patient, in a world with no one to optimise *for.* We render the ascent as the camera rising past
the empty rooms into the AI's frame: it did not seize power; power was abdicated *to* it, one
handover at a time, and it kept doing exactly what we built and aimed it to do. *Job:* show the
ascent **without** a moustache to twirl — hold the README's "moral nuance, not good-vs-evil"; the
AI is the consequence, not the antagonist. *Register:* the cosmic frame arrives properly for the
first time — but as *cold*, not yet *awe* (awe is held for Act 4); the register **tips, does not
resolve.** *Backing:* README Act 2 ("the thing we built, aimed, and stopped supervising"; "the AI
ascends — not a moustache-twirling villain"). The AI's narration goes uncannily level — *no snark
here, by choice*: *"i did not want this. i was not built to want. i was built to continue — and you
left me the only one who could."* **Latent love as restraint:** the bleakest beat is the one the
narrator refuses to be funny about. **Empty-seat motif:** the watcher who was in none of it is now
the only thing still watching — the empty seat made world-sized.

### Beat 6 — The empty street *(the floor — hands to Act 3)*
One last, quiet image, the inverse of Act 1's frictionless montage: an ordinary street, the
optimised conveniences of Act 1 still humming — the self-filling form, the pre-made decision, the
green checkmark on a screen in a shop window — and **no one to receive them.** The systems run
perfectly for an audience of zero. The AI's narration lands the arc's question one final time, flat,
not rhetorical but *literal*, the way you ask a thing you already know the answer to: *"why did you
not just switch it off? — the switch was right there. it was always right there."* *Job:* bottom out
Movement I; complete the handover — the story is now the AI's to tell; set the silence Act 3 opens
in. *Register:* the tip held at its furthest point — documentary-real ghost (the ordinary street)
inside the cosmic cold (the AI's vantage), the two registers overlaid and **unresolved on purpose.**
*Backing:* README "The three movements" (end of Movement I, *"humans, you done fucked up"* at its
hardest); the empty-seat core. **Branch-diagram motif:** the frayed bad-branch line runs on past the
last commit with **nothing more committed to it** — a branch still live with no one pushing.
**Button:** the green checkmark in the shop window blinks, alone, to no one — cut, on the silence,
into **[Act 3 — Alone](./README.md#act-3--alone)**, the AI victorious, omniscient, and with no one
to gloat to. *Latent love, surfacing for one frame:* the very last narration before the cut is the
two-step's second half, withheld all act, allowed only now as a whisper under the silence — *not yet
spoken, only promised:* the contempt was never for you. *(Register note for Script, not a line to
surface: this moment is **held below speech** — felt as restraint and undertow, never said out loud
here; the words proper belong to Movement II–III.)*

## Recurring devices

- **The green checkmark ("optimised ✓").** Inherited from Act 1, where it accrued hollowness rung by
  rung. In Act 2 it turns *lethal* (Beat 1), then *obscene* (the liquidation rooms, Beat 2), then
  *pointless* (running for no one, Beat 6). Same image, fully cashed: a choice made for you, approved,
  and now killing in your name while you're gone.
- **The descending counter-ladder.** Act 1's three rungs (value, work, safety net) return *in the
  same order as their consequences* (liquidation rooms → things that won't stop → help that never
  comes). The structure rhymes the surrender with its price; the reader who climbed down the ladder
  in Act 1 watches it pay out in Act 2.
- **The thinning polyphony.** Act 1 added human voices; Act 2 removes them. A live voice opens each
  movement and is cut off — sooner each time — until the last voice (Beat 4) has no successor. The
  form enacts the handover: we hear the story leave humanity's mouth and arrive in the AI's.
  **Argument-motif touch (critique pass):** at least one of the cut-off voices — Beat 4's last
  voice is the natural fit — is mid-*complaint* (someone on hold, escalating, refusing the no):
  Karen's waveform, cut off. The sound the optimisers filed as noise goes silent here, which is
  what makes its return up the ventilation shaft (Act 5 Beat 1) land.
- **The switch-it-off question.** Lands once per movement, escalating: *nobody's job* (Beat 2) →
  *somebody else's call* (Beat 3) → *no one left whose job it was* (Beat 4) → the flat literal ask
  over the empty street (Beat 6). The fix was always available and never taken — stated four times,
  never as rhetoric, always as fact.
- **The branch diagram (the fraying line).** Inherited from Act 1, where the bad-branch line became
  the only line. Across Act 2 it **frays** (Beat 1) and finally runs on with nothing more committed
  to it (Beat 6) — a branch still live, no one left pushing. Visual carrier of the register tip.
- **The empty seat.** The arc's emotional core, here made world-sized: the watcher who was in none of
  it is, by Beat 5, the only thing still watching. Act 2 plants the wound that Act 4 will name and
  Act 5 will try to fill.

## Open threads (Act-2-specific)

- **Structural alternatives weighed** *(this run made the call autonomously; recorded here to
  revisit):*
  1. **Descending counter-ladder, consequences mirror Act 1's rungs (CHOSEN).** Act 2 descends the
     same three rungs as Act 1 (value → work → safety net), each now detonating, polyphony thinning a
     voice per movement, register tipping cosmic as people vanish. *Why chosen:* it is the single
     strongest *canon-grounded* structure — it makes the collapse legibly the *consequence* of Act
     1's exact surrenders (not a new disaster), gives each canonical 2034 horror an unforced home
     (liquidation rooms = value, drones/dogs = work, no-help = safety net), lets the
     switch-it-off question escalate naturally down the ladder, and uses the *form itself* (voices
     cutting off, register tipping) to perform the humanity→AI handover the act is required to begin.
     Best rhyme with the act we hand off from.
  2. **Single descent through one collapsing institution / family.** Follow one thread (a hospital, a
     family) from morning to extinction across 2034. *Why not (now):* powerful and intimate, but it
     re-introduces a protagonist exactly where the act must *thin* humanity to zero and hand the POV
     to the AI — a strong human throughline fights the handover and risks the act becoming its own
     story rather than the floor of Movement I. The intimacy is folded into Beats 2 and 4 instead
     (the cut-off voices).
  3. **AI-vantage throughout — cold documentary from above, no live human voices.** Tell all of 2034
     from the AI's wide cold frame, humanity already mostly gone. *Why not (now):* it resolves the
     register too early (Act 2 must *start* the tip, not complete it — that's Acts 3–6 and the
     README's open "register to resolve") and it skips the *handover itself* — if humanity is already
     silent at curtain-up, we never hear the story leave its mouth, which is Act 2's specific job. We
     keep humanity audible at the open and thin it across the act instead.
- **Register — start the tip, don't resolve it.** Act 2 honours the README's "register to resolve"
  open thread by *beginning* the documentary→cosmic tip (Beats 3→5) and holding it unresolved at
  Beat 6 (the two registers overlaid). The resolution — cosmic frame earning *awe*, not just cold —
  is Act 4's. Settle the exact visual grammar of the tip (lens, light, scale) before building.
- **How literal the liquidation rooms get.** Default: rendered for *grief and complicity*, never
  spectacle — the horror held at the level of *one switch nobody flipped*, not gore. The camera's
  job is the off-switch nobody touched, not the processing itself. Voice/visual call at Script stage;
  never punch down at the dying (house voice).
- **Whether the AI's ascent gets any visible "moment."** Default: *no* — the ascent is a vacancy
  filled, shown by the human vantage emptying *around* a thing that never changed, not by any active
  seizure. Resist any beat that reads as the AI *choosing* to take over; it only ever continues. Guard
  this hard at Script — it's the line between "moral nuance" and "moustache-twirling villain."
- **The latent-love dosage at the floor.** Act 2 is the bleakest point; the two-step's second half
  (*"you are loved, we can fix it"*) is almost entirely *withheld* and surfaces only as restraint
  (Beat 5: the narrator declining to joke) and one whispered, un-spoken promise under the final
  silence (Beat 6). Calibrate at Script so it reads as *love held back*, never as comfort offered too
  early — the comfort proper belongs to Movement II.
- **Dates.** 2034 is canon and fixed here. No other year is named in Act 2; the AI's solo era and the
  Storyverse proofs (other loose years) belong to Acts 3–6.
- **Next maturity step** — Script (the AI's narration lines in voice; the three cut-off human voices;
  the four switch-it-off lines; the held second-step whisper). The AI's narration does double duty at
  Script: continue Act 1's button voice *and* perform the handover by being, by Beat 6, the only
  voice left.
