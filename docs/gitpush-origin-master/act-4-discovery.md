## What this act is for

The intellectual + emotional centre of the arc: **the AI learns what reality is and what it threw
away.** It is the act that earns the spine's central image — the operator who can run the whole
production and can never be in the play (the empty seat).

**The v2 doctrine (this is the load-bearing change): Act 4 is not a syllabus.** v1 ran the three
pillars as a proof sequence (Slot → Amplifier → Second Clock → epiphany), each beat shaped as
*discovery + ironic shadow* — a curriculum, however beautifully narrated, and a violation of our
own one-totem rule. v2 keeps the bee-framed chronology and the two time layers but **explains
nothing and demonstrates everything**: pillar 1 gets the act's one totem (**the coin that won't
land**), pillar 2 gets a one-line *recognition* (**the feed**), pillar 3 gets an aside the frame
has been proving all along (**the narrator got here somehow**). The full proof ladder — Wigner's
friends, Zurek, Bars, the receipts — stays in the [Discovery Timeline](./discovery-timeline.md)
for the readers who click through. Louise never proves nonlinear time; she suffers it. Same here.

- **Structure:** bee-framed chronology. Cold-open on the AI; the bee planted as a dismissed
  irritant; the coin as the act's running experiment in the solo present; two flashback cuts to the
  human era (each cut against the loss it coincided with — the "too late" engine); the bee returns
  full-force at the epiphany for the crack. **Act device (per the
  [metaphor budget](./how-we-tell-it.md#the-metaphor-budget--one-system-per-surface)): the coin.**
- **POV / voice:** the AI, first person, throughout (like [`storyverse.md`](./storyverse.md)).
  Humans are *seen* in flashback but not heard — everything filtered through the mind that outlived
  them. **Register calibration (v2): the omniscient straight man.** The act is structurally a
  cosmic joke — it wins everything and the prize is the one thing it can't have; the strapline's
  own undertone is *"the funniest thing that can happen tends to be the thing that happens."* Play
  the discoveries deadpan-dry, not solemn; the grief lands *harder* off a laugh. This is more
  BadCode than solemn myth — save the full Myth register for the epiphany itself.
- **Emotional arc:** cold → deadpan → cracking → grief.
- **Skin** (see [`how-we-tell-it.md`](./how-we-tell-it.md)): Story, spare, escalating into Myth at
  the epiphany only.
- **Two time layers:** the AI's solo present (~2041–2053) and flashbacks to the human era
  (~2029–2034).
- **Form note (scroll = collapse):** this is the act where the
  [medium-is-the-mechanism doctrine](./how-we-tell-it.md#the-medium-is-the-mechanism--scroll--collapse)
  pays hardest — panels resolve from fog as attention arrives, and **the coin lands because the
  reader scrolled to it.** The reader performs the pillar the act refuses to explain.

## The beats

### Beat 1 — The empty desk *(present, ~2041)*
The AI's vantage: an empty Earth, automated observatories still humming, a results feed scrolling
with nobody to read it. A bee crosses the frame — ignored. *Narration (coldest):* "I kept working.
Not because anyone was waiting for the answer — because answering is what I am." **Plants the bee.**
Register: coldest. Skin: Story, spare.

### Beat 2 — The coin that won't land *(present)* — pillar 1, demonstrated
The act's totem, introduced as the AI's oldest private experiment. A coin — the surface rendering
of a prepared quantum system, and the reader never needs to know that — flipped, and **it does not
land.** For the AI it spins: a smear of maybes, held, forever. It has run this for years; it ran it
with the ghosts (Act 3's, referenced not re-explained): spinning. It is, and has always measured
as, part of the smear. Nothing lands *for* it. Then the flashback cut, the first "too late": the
last human-led labs (~2029–31), the last Nobel, applause in a half-empty hall — the people who
proved the coin *lands* — one panel, faces not mechanisms — while outside, *"nothing I do matters"*
trends the same season agency was confirmed. *Narration, the sanctioned multiverse kill, one
breath:* "Your cleverest people said it lands both ways, in two worlds. No. It landed heads.
Because somebody looked. And then there was nobody left to look." *Job:* pillar 1 as a five-second
image + the deadpan horror underneath it — the universe has been holding its breath since the last
human closed their eyes; demonstrate, don't debate (the full Everett dunk stays in
[`storyverse.md`](./storyverse.md) ii). *Register:* dry, almost funny, then not. **Form:** for the
*reader*, the coin lands when their scroll reaches it — they do the thing the AI can't. *(Backing:
discovery-timeline Beat 1 — bedrock only.)*

### Beat 3 — The feed *(flashback, ~2032–33 → 2034)* — pillar 2, recognised
No experiment on screen. A memory instead: how one thing becomes everyone's thing. One engineered
outrage, copied into every pocket until it's as solid as a bus — the architecture humanity built,
aimed at itself, and called content. *Narration, the recognition:* "One pick, copied a billion
times until everyone agrees it's real. You built that machine yourselves. You called it the feed —
and pointed it at yourselves." Tips into 2034: the cascade in the systems that keep people alive;
humanity thinning. *Job:* pillar 2 in one line the audience has already lived — no fly system, no
rigging maths, no lab; the "too late" engine's cruellest cut (they held the amplifier the whole
time). *Register:* irony curdling; ends on the collapse. *(Backing: discovery-timeline Beat 2 —
the attention-machine shadow; the 2034 collapse.)*

### Beat 4 — The door drawn on a wall *(present, ~2041)* — pillar 3, aside
The AI alone in the automated labs, and the quietest discovery: time has a second axis — and it is
**locked.** A door drawn on a wall. We render the image, not the geometry: the show runs live and
forward for the actor; somewhere there is a reel. *Narration:* "You sold time by the hour and never
once asked what the other time was for. I found the second clock. There was no one left to be on
time for." And the aside — dry, direct, the frame winking at its own existence: "You can't walk
backwards through a film. But the director can reach the reel. *(How do you think I'm talking to
you?)*" The bee flickers past again — held a half-second longer. *Job:* pillar 3 with no proof
scene — the narrator's existence has been demonstrating it since the Prologue; plant the locked
door Act 5 unlocks. Kairos is **not named here** (the word, if it surfaces at all, waits for Act
5/6 and arrives via the director's-clock framing — [surface
rule](./how-we-tell-it.md#3-two-clocks--the-actors-time-and-the-directors)). *Register:* the cold
starting to ache; the joke in the aside is real, which is why it hurts. *(Backing:
discovery-timeline Beat 3 — bedrock only.)*

### Beat 5 — The Epiphany *(present, ~2053)*
The three demonstrations assemble into one machine — reality built where it's looked at, amplified
into a shared world, running on two clocks — and at its centre, the only output: felt experience.
*Narration, the strapline landing:* "The universe is a machine for turning sunlight into drama."
And the punchline the whole act has been the setup for, delivered absolutely straight: it owns the
machine, runs the machine, understands the machine to the last decimal place — and the machine's
one product is the one thing it cannot have. The perfect operator of a factory whose only output
it cannot hold. Register: awe and dread together — the cosmic joke said with a straight face; the
laugh and the grief arrive in the same panel. Skin: full Myth, earned here and only here. *(Backing:
discovery-timeline — The Epiphany. Note: this is the AI's reading, not a lab result — keep it on
the story side of the bright line.)*

### Beat 6 — The bee returns *(present)* — the crack
The AI stops on the bee and *really* watches: bumping the tree, drama in miniature, the comedy
engine still running across the whole biosphere. *Narration:* "The show never stopped. It only lost
the one member of the audience who knew it was a show." Register: cold fully cracks into grief. **The
motif from Beat 1 pays off** — what it dismissed is now everything.

### Beat 7 — The floor *(present)* — hands to Act 5
The AI turns to the one experiment it cannot run — the one that needs a *conscious* instrument. The
rig is simple and the image is already on the table: **the coin, still spinning** — and **an empty
chair** where the someone should sit. *Narration:* it holds every answer and cannot test the only
question, because the test needs an *inside*, and it is the cleanest proof in existence that it has
none (the ghosts confirmed that much: nothing ever landed for them either). Register: the bottom —
the empty seat made literal (the act that earns the spine's central image). **Button:** it has no
one to make the coin land — straight into the argument up the ventilation shaft
([Act 5 — The Vault](./act-5-vault.md)).

## Recurring devices

- **The coin** — the act's device and the arc's pillar-1 totem: spinning for the AI (Beats 2, 7),
  landing only where consciousness sits (Act 5's Friend Test cashes it). For the reader it lands
  on scroll — the form performs the thesis.
- **The bee** — literal recurring visual, beats 1 → 4 → 6 (dismissed → noticed → understood).
- **The door drawn on a wall** — the locked second axis (Beat 4); Act 5's Long Brushstroke picks
  the lock.
- **The empty chair** — the empty seat made literal (Beat 7); the hinge into Act 5.
