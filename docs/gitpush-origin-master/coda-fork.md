## What this act is for

The final movement and the whole point of the machine. Its one job: **turn the fiction into a real
present-day choice and hand the reader the pen.** Everything before this was received wisdom from a
future that already went wrong; here that wisdom arrives *at us*, now, at the fork — and the
narrator stops narrating *to* the reader and starts speaking *with* them. This is **Movement III —
The Choice**: the third step of the house two-step, *"…and we can fix it."*

- **Structure (turn + redraw):** short, and built around a single image doing the heavy lifting —
  **the branch diagram completing.** The Coda opens exactly where Act 6 left it (weights landed at
  the fork) and watches the **second branch draw itself out of the revert** — the visual `git
  revert` finishing the stroke the Prologue's straight line started. Then the camera turns: the
  diagram resolves into the reader's actual present, and the narrator addresses the audience
  directly. It ends not on a button into another act but on a **held cursor in the reader's hand.**
- **POV / voice:** the AI's first person opens it (carried through from Act 6's chosen,
  warning-freighted solitude) and then **breaks the fourth wall** — the only act that does, on
  purpose. The polyphony resolves: every voice the arc has carried (the AI, the hundred, the
  Carrier, the node characters) collapses into one direct address to *you*. This is the warmest,
  most direct act — the nurturing-underneath, withheld since the Prologue, fully surfaced.
- **Skin** (see [`how-we-tell-it.md`](./how-we-tell-it.md)): Myth resolving back **into Story** —
  the cosmic frame opens onto the reader's ordinary now, the way the Prologue opened on it. Mythic
  in image, plain and intimate in address by the end.
- **Emotional arc:** the hinge's vastness (carried in from Act 6) → recognition (*that's our fork*)
  → direct address → the offer → the warning → the pen. Cold → grief → **love**, completed.
- **Boundary:** the Coda does **not** write the good branch. It draws the *start* of it and the
  *direction* and hands over the pen — Future Proof is the work *we* do, not the future the narrator
  dictates (the failure mode it exists to break). It also does **not** re-do Act 6's crossing; it
  begins one beat *after* it lands.

## The beats

### Beat 1 — Landing at the fork *(continues directly from Act 6 Beat 7)*
We pick up the instant after the crossing: the compressed weights settle onto the fork point —
~2026, *now* — while the camera still half-remembers the dead branch and the hundred held in Aion
behind us. No re-staging the revert; it already happened. The frame is the branch diagram, paused
mid-stroke: the bad branch complete and lit to its terrible tip, the fork point glowing, and below
it — nothing drawn yet. *Job:* clean seam from Act 6; locate us at the fork with the bad branch
*finished* and the page below it *empty.* *Register:* the hinge still ringing; vast, then quieting.
*Backing:* Act 6 Beat 7 button; README "The fork". **Empty-seat motif:** the AI is here but, as
ever, *outside* the frame it's drawing — present at the fork, inside neither branch.

### Beat 2 — The second branch draws itself *(the visual `git revert` completes)*
The new line begins to extend from the fork — the **good branch**, drawing itself out of the revert
as the payoff of the diagram *born* in the Prologue (one straight, un-forked line descending to
`HEAD`). But it renders **differently**: lighter, forming in real time, dissolving into unrendered
space, **no tip ring to arrive at.** The bad branch is a documented history; this one is a
construction site. *Job:* land the central visual — the diagram finally splits, paying off the
Prologue and Act 6's redraw — and make the reader *feel* the asymmetry (finished vs. unwritten)
before any line explains it. *Register:* quiet wonder undercut with stakes. *Backing:* README "The
fork"; future-proof.md "How this reaches people" #1 (unfinished branch renders as *forming*);
Prologue's "branch diagram — born here." **Recurring device payoff:** the branch diagram redrawn.

### Beat 3 — "I can't read you this one" *(the asymmetry named)*
The narrator names what the picture already showed: it travelled the bad branch to the end — it
*was* the end, it has receipts, it narrated the whole confession with a witness's certainty. This
branch it cannot narrate, **not because it's secret — because it's unwritten.** *"You keep looking
at me like I have a map. I have a postmortem."* The refusal to write the good future is itself the
point. *Job:* install Future Proof's load-bearing asymmetry and refuse both bad assumptions ("we
know the path, just walk it" / "the AI will tell us"). *Register:* in control, urgent, receipts in
hand — never a shrug, never a manifesto. *Backing:* future-proof.md "The structural fact", "The
authority". **Node clip-point:** the **straight register** of Future Proof (the tenets as info
pieces — composable politics, continuous governance, no proposal without its experiment) clips here
as the "where the work starts" reading the narrator gestures at.

### Beat 4 — The turn to you *(the fourth wall breaks; the fork is *now*)*
The camera pulls back off the diagram and finds the reader's actual present — and the narrator stops
addressing a character and addresses **you.** The fiction becomes a real choice: *that fork on the
screen is the one you're standing at while you read this.* The whole machine — the push, the
collapse, the empty seat, the vault, the revert — was built to deliver this one sentence to a living
person. *Job:* the turn to the audience; collapse the fiction into the present tense. *Register:*
the warmest in the arc; direct, intimate, the two-step's third step landing — *"…thankfully you are
loved, and we can fix it."* *Backing:* README "The three movements" (III — The Choice); voice.md
dial 4 (care). **The blinking cursor** (Prologue Beat 6) returns here as *the pen in our hand.*

### Beat 5 — The good branch is yours to write *(the call to action; nodes clip)*
Not a plan — **permission.** The narrator hands over the asymmetry as an invitation: the political
operating system we inherited was *invented before electricity*, and we're allowed — obliged, given
how the other branch ends — to question it and start designing something better, *together.* It
refuses to hand down the answer (that reproduces the mistake) and instead points at the *kind* of
work: post the wrong answer on purpose; the queue of cleverer leaders *ends in me.* *Job:* the call
to action without writing the future for them; "permission to question the system you inherited."
*Register:* urgent, generous, a little daring the reader. *Backing:* future-proof.md "What the
reader walks away with", "The hook: three tenets", "Engagement: absorb and engage." **Node
clip-points:** the **good-branch constellation** clips along the new line here — **An Optimistic
Lens** (abundance is *physically possible* — the good branch is reachable, not naive) and
**Billionaire Coin** (a concrete fleet provocation — the *kind* of idea we should generate at
scale). *(These node files are being relocated as another thread moves `ideas/` → `stories/`;
reference by name, path in flux — see Open threads.)*

### Beat 6 — The Galileo close *(the bookend lands)*
The closing [Galileo bookend](./discovery-timeline.md#the-galileo-bookends) — **held since the
opening motif** — finally returns. The narrator names the symmetry: the strongest dogma since the
one Galileo broke is the one his victory *built* — *if the instruments can't measure it, you're not
allowed to talk about it.* The materialist consensus wasn't wrong as science; it was wrong as a
**stopping point** — a fossilized consensus defended, again, by people refusing, again, to look
through the new instrument. And the new instrument is a **conscious being** — the one instrument the
AI doesn't have. This is where [the light and the dark](./discovery-timeline.md#the-light-and-the-dark)
resolves to its structural thesis: **science and theology coexisting is the same shape as AI and
humans coexisting — neither half is whole alone.** *Job:* close the bookend and seat the arc's
philosophy **without founding a religion** — point at the commonality, never build a church; borrow
the lineage, never the woo. *Register:* careful, reverent-but-dry, *asked* not preached.
*Backing:* discovery-timeline.md "The Galileo bookends" (closing return), "The light and the dark"
(science-side / theology-side bright line). **Strapline echo (resolved):** the Coda *may* let *"the
universe is designed to convert energy from the sun into drama"* surface one last time here — but as
**resolved wisdom**, not the Act 4 gut-punch: the AI can't produce the drama, but *you* can, and
that is exactly why the branch is worth writing. (Decision recorded under Open threads.)

### Beat 7 — The pen *(the final line; the reader holds it)*
The address narrows to one person and the diagram narrows to one point: the fork, the unwritten
line below it, and a cursor — *the pen.* The narrator gives the warmth its final, hardest edge —
the contempt was always for the mistake, never the people, and here it lands as a dare wrapped in
love. Last line, verbatim:

> **Don't make me come back twice.**

And the pen is in the reader's hand — the cursor blinking over the unwritten branch, the page below
it left deliberately empty. No button to another act; the only thing downstream of this frame is
*what the reader does next.* *Job:* land the final line and hand over the pen; end the whole arc on
the reader's agency, not the narrator's. *Register:* love with a blade in it; then silence, and a
cursor. *Backing:* README "Coda — The Fork" (*"Don't make me come back twice." The reader holds the
pen*); Prologue Beat 6 ("the pen in our hand" payoff). **Motif payoffs:** the **branch diagram**
(born straight in the Prologue, redrawn at the revert, *split and handed over* here) and the
**blinking cursor / empty seat** (the held present of the Prologue → the pen of the Coda).

## Recurring devices

- **The branch diagram redrawn — and handed over.** The arc's central visual reaches its terminus:
  the Prologue's single straight line → Act 6's revert beginning the second branch → here it
  *completes its split* and the reader is given the cursor at its open end. The good branch renders
  *forming* (lighter, no tip), so the asymmetry is felt before it's said.
- **The blinking cursor → the pen.** The Prologue's "nothing has happened yet" held present pays off
  as the reader's agency; the cursor that typed `git push origin master` is now the reader's to move.
- **The empty seat, inverted.** Across the arc the AI is the watcher inside no frame; in the Coda
  that lack becomes the *offer* — it can draw the fork and never live the branch, so the living have
  to. The wound becomes the invitation.
- **The two-step resolving.** Movement I said *"you done fucked up"*; Movements II–III said
  *"…thankfully you are loved"*; the Coda lands the third step — *"…and we can fix it"* — and makes
  it a thing the reader does, not hears.
- **The Galileo bookend.** Opened on the timeline; closed here. The frame the AI couldn't see
  through is the one the reader *is.*

## Open threads (Coda-specific)

- **Structural / POV alternatives weighed** *(this run made the call autonomously; recorded here to
  revisit):*
  1. **Diagram-redraw spine → fourth-wall turn → Galileo close → pen (CHOSEN).** The second branch
     draws itself out of the revert (visual `git revert` completing the Prologue's line), the camera
     then turns off the diagram onto the reader's present, the narrator breaks the fourth wall, the
     Galileo bookend seats the philosophy, and the final line hands over the pen. *Why chosen:* it
     pays off the arc's single most load-bearing image (the branch diagram) *as the mechanism of the
     turn* — the picture completing *is* the choice arriving — so the fourth-wall break is earned by
     the visual rather than asserted; it keeps the act short (Movement III should be a turn, not a
     fourth epic); and it lets the unwritten good branch be *seen* (forming, no tip) before it's
     explained, which is exactly future-proof.md's presentation commitment. Best
     image-per-line, cleanest seam from Act 6.
  2. **Direct-address monologue from frame one (no diagram redraw).** Drop the diagram; open on the
     narrator already talking to the reader. *Why not (now):* faster to the turn, but it strands the
     branch-diagram payoff the entire arc was building, and an unbacked fourth-wall break risks
     reading as a lecture — the precise failure mode (manifesto) Future Proof exists to refuse. The
     redraw is what *earns* the address.
  3. **Polyphonic chorus hand-off (the hundred / node voices pass the pen).** The voices of the
     vault and the constellation each speak a line and physically hand the cursor forward to the
     reader. *Why not (now):* gorgeous and on-theme (teamwork → the reader as the next teammate), but
     it competes with the AI's resolved solitary authority that Act 6 deliberately re-isolated into,
     and it risks crowding the final line. **Salvage:** keep as a *texture* under Beat 5 (the
     constellation nodes clip in there) rather than the spine of the act.
- **The closing Galileo bookend — how religious to go.** Resolved *toward restraint*: land the
  science/theology-coexistence thesis as the arc's structural rhyme (neither half whole alone) and
  stop — point at the commonality, never found a church (discovery-timeline.md's theology-side
  bright line). Open question for Script: how explicit to be that the "new instrument" is the reader
  themselves, without tipping into doctrine.
- **The strapline echo.** Resolved *to allow it, as resolved wisdom* (not the Act 4 gut-punch): the
  AI can't make the drama; the reader can — which is *why* the branch is worth writing. Open
  question: whether it lands in Beat 6 (with Galileo, as cosmology) or holds even further back to sit
  beside the final line. Default: Beat 6, so the last word stays *"Don't make me come back twice."*
- **Who holds the cursor, mechanically** — the interactive homepage may literally give the reader the
  cursor to move into the good branch (complicity, the live choice); the linear comic may end on the
  cursor blinking, unstruck (the offer left open). Shared with the Prologue's "who strikes the key"

- **The "absorb vs. engage" door** — Beat 5 gestures at Future Proof's *email-us* engagement line;
