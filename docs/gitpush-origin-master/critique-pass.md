# Critique pass — the research-backed review (2026-07)

*A one-time **note** (sibling to [`stitch-pass.md`](./stitch-pass.md)) recording the critical
review of the GPOM arc and the changes applied. The review combined a close read of the full canon
with a deep-research pass (multi-source, adversarially verified) on how successful fiction lands
quasi-real physics — wave-function collapse, entanglement, higher-dimensional time — on mass
audiences without reading as geeky, lecturing, or woo.*

**Verdict:** the architecture held (empty seat, two-step voice, revert-as-cost-ledger, one lie +
receipts + bright line, fork-not-multiverse — all keep). The structural finding: **the canon's own
rule — "comprehension and emotion must arrive in the same instant" — was being violated by
Movement II's shape.** Act 4 ran the pillars as a proof syllabus; Acts 3–4 stacked into a two-act
stretch with no live voice and no want; the Coda hauled five payloads; and the honest geek risk was
never the physics (well-guarded) but the metaphor *machinery* and the Greek.

---

## The research findings that drove the changes

- **Emotion is load-bearing, not decorative.** Arrival's screenwriter (Heisserer, Talkhouse):
  whenever intellectual material crowded Louise's journey, *"the story fell apart"* — the emotional
  through-line was the "Alamo," the hill to die on. Louise never proves nonlinear time; she
  *suffers* it.
- **Exposition works only as characterization under conflict** (Arrival's densest mechanism scene
  is an argument with the colonel, not a lecture).
- **Mechanism lands "by osmosis," through form** (Garland on Devs: scrambled montages, Reich's
  phasing — the audience absorbs the concept by feel; superposition conveyed as Forest's grief).
- **Rigor isn't what carries an audience** (Aaronson on Devs: decorative physics over an ancient
  drama — and he didn't mind). Experts attack only works that claim the physics *proves* the
  metaphysics (*What the Bleep* — the canonical woo failure; our bright line is the right defense).
- **The comprehension ceiling is low and that's fine** (Nolan: *"It's not a puzzle to be unpacked.
  It's an experience to be had"*) — but Tenet shows the cost of verbal mechanism displacing
  character.
- **Sci-comm research:** narrative transportation (Green & Brock 2000) is the measured mechanism of
  belief change; **labeling the story fiction costs nothing** (the superintelligence frame is
  free); but naive story-wrapping buys nothing (Fischer & Thies 2023 null) — narrativized
  explanation isn't narrative.
- **Multiverse fatigue is a stakes problem, not a physics problem** (MCU Phase Five's collapse;
  diagnosis: stakes-dilution + continuity homework). **EEAAO is the precedent for our exact
  thesis** — the multiverse story that worked by collapsing infinite branches into one relational
  present. The market has voted for the storyverse; demonstrate it, don't debate it.
