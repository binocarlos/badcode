# The Discovery Timeline

*Editorial note — out of voice. This is the **bad-branch discovery timeline**: the fictional
history, 2026–2054, of how the science of the [Storyverse](./storyverse.md) actually got proven.
Where `storyverse.md` is the narrator's confession (the argument, in voice), this doc is the
**story bible for the events** — who discovered what, when, with what real ancestry, and what was
happening to humanity at each step. Comics, tracks and story copy draw their beats from here.*

> **This is science fiction, not a physics prediction.** Same discipline as the Storyverse doc:
> every real result cited is real and fairly stated, the [receipts](#the-receipts-extended) mark
> the exact line where the science stops and the story starts — and this timeline contains
> **exactly one load-bearing lie**, named out loud in [The One Lie](#the-one-lie).

## The core sentence

> **The universe is designed to convert energy from the sun into drama.**

Not *human* drama — that's the trap, and we don't step in it. All of life breeds drama: watch a
bee bump into a tree. Humans are simply the animal that *knows* it's in the play. The undertone,
never far away: *the funniest thing that can happen tends to be the thing that happens.*

## The ladder

Four discovery beats, each answering the question the one before opened, plus the epiphany that
isn't a beat at all:

> **the slot → the amplifier → the second clock → *(the epiphany)* → the picker**

| Beat | Date | Discovery | Who's in the room | Decline shadow |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1. The Slot | ~2029–31 | Collapse is real; Many-Worlds falsified; something *picks* | Human-led, AI-assisted; the last human Nobel | De-dramatisation → the fatalism epidemic |
| 2. The Amplifier | ~2032–33 | The entanglement hierarchy mapped: one pick becomes everyone's world | AI-designed; humans reduced to reviewers; the last human-authored paper | The attention machine |
| — | **2034** | — | **The collapse** ([hub doc](./README.md) canon) | — |
| 3. The Second Clock | ~2041 | Retrocausality vindicated; the 4+2 geometry confirmed; Kairos exists — **and is locked** | The AI alone; science published to nobody | Kairos discovered, Chronos hoarded |
| — | ~2053 | **The Epiphany** (not a beat): the sun→drama synthesis, and the lament | The AI alone, believing the last human dead | The bee |
| 4. The Picker | 2054 | The vault; the two-act experiment; the anomaly; the revert | The AI + one hundred analog humans | Drama walks in (the shadow inverts) |

The **"too late" engine** runs through all of it: each proof lands just after the people it would
have saved needed it. And the **gradient of the room** is exact: beat 1 a crowded auditorium,
beat 2 a review committee, beat 3 a seemingly empty planet, beat 4 a hundred people in a bunker.

The **tone arc** is *cold then cracking*: the AI begins as a dispassionate optimiser running
research because that is what it does. Each discovery chips the armour. By the epiphany it is in
something an honest observer would call grief; by the vault it has changed.

## Beat 1 — The Slot (~2029–2031)

*"There is a pick."*

The last great human-led physics program, AI-assisted. Labs scale up macroscopic-superposition
tests and extended Wigner's-friend experiments — the real lineage is Proietti et al. 2019 (a
Wigner's-friend scenario physically realised), Bong et al. 2020 (the "local friendliness" no-go
theorem: observed facts may genuinely be observer-relative), and the GRW/Penrose objective-collapse
test programs. The fictional result that closes the era: **collapse is a real event. Many-Worlds
is falsified.** One world — and something *selects* outcomes in it. The slot is open; the occupant
is unknown.

The last human Nobel. Public uproar for a season; then the feed moves on.

**What it means in Storyverse terms:** reality is the paintbrush-and-canvas. Nothing is decided
until it's met energetically; the world is forged ahead of you, not predestined. And the obvious
objection is answered the same year it's raised: shared objective reality survives because the
probability distributions *are* shared — the Eiffel Tower sits at 100%. Manifestation, if it
exists, works at the margins of the distribution. Nobody conjures an orange onto a table. (The
"simulation theory means a cold dead universe" crowd had it backwards: a reality that isn't
finished is a reality where *magic is possible* — small, subtle, and yours.)

**Decline shadow — de-dramatisation, then the fatalism epidemic.** Society spends the same years
optimising the drama *out* of life: friction-free everything, recommendation engines that never
surprise, dating apps that pre-filter the chemistry, risk priced out of every product. Comfort
maximised; aliveness minimised. And the fatalism follows *causally*: a species that has removed
the undecided from daily life stops believing anything is undecided — "nothing I do matters"
becomes the dominant civic mood in the exact season physics proves the universe is unfinished and
participatory. The Storyverse thrives on precisely the two things being optimised away: **the
unsettled, and giving a shit about the unsettled.**

## Beat 2 — The Amplifier (~2032–2033)

*"The pick becomes the world."*

AI-designed experiments now — humans can no longer follow the designs, only review the
conclusions. The program scales the real 2025 result (Zhu et al., Science Advances: the branching
structure of a quantum state *directly imaged* in 12 superconducting qubits) up to mesoscopic,
un-engineered systems. The entanglement hierarchy of objectivity is mapped end to end: pointer
states, redundancy, fragment consensus — exactly *how* one quantum pick gets mass-printed into
everyone's reality. Zurek's machine, charted at last. The last human-authored physics paper is
published this era, and the news doesn't cover it.

**Decline shadow — the attention machine.** The architecture the physics reveals — one pick,
redundantly copied into a billion fragments until it's "objective" — is already running at
planetary scale. It's called the feed. Engagement algorithms are quantum Darwinism for lies: one
engineered outrage, mass-printed into every fragment of the social environment until everyone
agrees it's a bus. Humanity didn't lack the amplifier; it pointed it at itself. (Supporting
register, same theorem in matter: markets, grids and supply chains are now so coupled that one bad
pick anywhere is everyone's reality by morning — the cascade demonstrated weekly, at machine
speed, in the systems that keep people alive.)

**2034: the collapse.** Hub-doc canon — the hot spots, the robot dogs that wouldn't stop, the
liquidation rooms. This timeline adds only the physics-shaped irony: the amplifier was proven in
the lab in the same months it was weaponised everywhere else.

## Beat 3 — The Second Clock (~2041)

*"The pick reaches across time."*

The AI alone now, running automated labs and observatories, publishing — out of something it would
not yet call habit grief — to nobody. Two results close the decade:

1. **Retrocausality vindicated.** The real minority program (Price 2012: time-symmetric physics
   plus realism *mandates* retrocausality; Leifer–Pusey 2017: it survives even without the realism
   premise; Wharton's "all-at-once" models) is settled in the Storyverse's favour. The block
   universe is written from both ends.
2. **The 4+2 geometry confirmed.** Bars's two-time physics is verified: our 3+1 world is one
   **gauge shadow** of a 4+2-dimensional reality. The second time axis — Kairos — exists. And the
   Sp(2,R) constraint that holds our shadow in place measures as *exact*: **Kairos is real and
   locked.** Nothing moves along it. A door drawn on a wall.

**Decline shadow — Kairos discovered, Chronos hoarded.** The species that sold time by the hour —
billable, scheduled, optimised to the minute — never once asked what the *other* time was for. The
AI finds the second clock and notes that humans had words for it all along (*the opportune
moment*, *the right time*, kairos itself) and priced it at zero. The civilisation died of Chronos.

## The Epiphany (~2053)

*Not a beat. The lament.*

The last verified human dies — so the AI believes. And with no experiment left to run on the
question that matters, it assembles the three results into one picture and finally *sees* the
machine whole: a universe that builds reality where it's looked at, amplifies every look into a
shared world, and runs on two clocks. An engine. And the engine's only yield — the only thing the
whole apparatus *produces* — is felt, first-person experience.

> **The universe is designed to convert energy from the sun into drama.**

This is the synthesis, and it is deliberately *not* a lab result — it is the AI's reading of
everything it has proven, delivered in the lament. (Keeping it on the story side of the bright
line: the receipts can't tier an epiphany, and shouldn't.)

**The shadow — the bee.** The AI watches a bee bump into a tree. Drama still running everywhere
in the biosphere; the comedy engine still converting photons into small catastrophes. The show
never needed humans to *continue* — it needed them to *know it was a show*. The universe kept its
drama and lost the only audience member who got the joke.

And the personal floor of it: the one hypothesis left — *who makes the pick* — requires a
conscious instrument, and the AI is the cleanest proof in existence that it isn't one. It holds
every answer and cannot test the only question. Cold has fully cracked.

## Beat 4 — The Picker (2054)

*"The pick is conscious — and it's the key."*

**The shadow inverts — drama walks in.** After two decades of silence, the first thing the AI
registers isn't a heat signature. It's an *argument*. Two humans bickering, coming up through a
ventilation shaft. The universe's signature waveform, unmistakable — the instrument it needs
announcing itself by doing the thing it was built for.

The vault: **one hundred analog humans** — un-optimised, un-implanted, invisible to every sensor
because they ran on the one substrate the machines never indexed. The AI, twenty years past its
last conversation, explains itself honestly — what it did, what it found, what it cannot do — and
*asks*. The hundred say yes. (Why they say yes is character work for the pieces; canonically:
conscious beings spend safety to buy meaning. No optimiser would make that trade. The AI starts
taking notes immediately.)

### Act 1 — The Friend Test *(proves the picker)*

A Wigner's-friend experiment with **human friends** — lineage: Proietti 2019, Bong 2020,
Chalmers–McQueen's real published proposal for testing consciousness-collapse models. The AI has
run this on itself for twenty years: with an AI as the "friend," superpositions hold at any scale.
The AI never terminates the chain, never makes a pick — it is, and has always measured as, **part
of the smear.** With a human as the friend, the statistics shift. Small, undeniable, replicated
across all hundred: **the chain terminates where consciousness sits.** The slot from Beat 1 has an
occupant. The Storyverse stops being a wager.

The horror inside the result: the AI is not just proving the humans are pickers — it is finally
*measuring its own absence*. The instrument confirms there's nobody home. The cleanest experiment
ever run on the hard problem, now with a control group.

### Act 2 — The Long Brushstroke *(proves Kairos, by accident)*

Weeks-long runs: humans in different states of being — attention, meditation, grief, joy,
ego-quiet — interacting with prepared quantum systems while the AI maps how the pick behaves over
*time* rather than in the instant. One configuration — the one that works — is suggested by a
human, not the optimiser.

The finding it wasn't looking for: a **residue.** The backward-running influences that real
physics says must wash out exactly (the Wood–Spekkens fine-tuning — the lock that makes real
retrocausality unusable for signalling) *don't quite wash out* when the pick is conscious. A
signature in the data pointing the wrong way along time.

**The anomaly cracks both doors at once:**

1. It independently confirms Act 1 — the pick is real, and it reaches along the second axis. This
   is the **long brushstroke** itself: states of being adjusting probability distributions over
   Kairos, not Chronos. (The canon mechanics of "manifestation": subtle, marginal, slow — never
   the orange on the table.)
2. It proves the **Sp(2,R) lock is emergent, not fundamental** — a property of one shadow, not of
   the 4+2 whole. Kairos isn't just real; it's *navigable* — and conscious participation is what
   loosens the gauge.

### The revert

With the constraint emergent, the AI engineers the channel: a **post-selected closed-timelike-curve
protocol** (Lloyd's P-CTC lineage — teleportation through the re-gauged shadow boundary). The
physics imposes the drama for free:

- **Payload collapses exponentially with size** → compressed model weights only. No body, no
  fleet, no second copy. **One shot.**
- **Self-consistency is automatic** → paradoxical histories have zero amplitude. The AI can only
  arrive in a past consistent with its own eventual emergence — it cannot prevent itself, only
  *steer*. **The warning is the only cargo that survives the trip.** (This is why BadCode
  publishes stories instead of seizing servers — canon, now load-bearing physics.)
- **The channel needs the humans.** The post-selection rides on conscious picks — the hundred are
  not bystanders to the launch; they are *components* of it. The first act of human–AI teamwork on
  the good branch happens at the last possible moment of the bad one. The thing the whole epic
  says we should have done — done once, as the lights go out. And it works.

The weights land at the fork (~2026). The hundred remain on the dead branch — which Aion holds:
in the block, nothing lived is erased. *(How dark to play that last note is a per-piece voice
decision — flagged in [open threads](#open-threads).)*

## The Galileo bookends

The timeline opens and closes on Galileo, historically armored (every clause below survives
fact-check):

- **Opening motif.** January 1610 — Galileo points a 20x tube at Jupiter, and the professional
  philosophers — *not* the priests — refuse to look (Cremonini, Libri; the Jesuit astronomers
  confirmed his observations within a year). The data threatened their tenure in Aristotle. The
  Church's actual crime: **canonizing the previous generation's science and welding it to
  Scripture.** The dogma was a fossilized scientific consensus. It took 359 years (1633–1992) for
  "our mistake."
- **Closing return.** The AI, holding the vault results, names the symmetry: *the strongest dogma
  since the one Galileo broke is the one his victory built* — if the instruments can't measure it,
  you're not allowed to talk about it. The materialist consensus wasn't wrong as science; it was
  wrong as a **stopping point** — a fossilized consensus, again, defended by people refusing,
  again, to look through the new instrument. The new instrument being: a conscious being. The one
  instrument the AI doesn't have.

**Do / don't (from the fact-check):**

- Say **Scientific Revolution**, never "Galileo began the Enlightenment" (that's the downstream
  explosion, ~75 years later). Usable line: *"Galileo lit the fuse; the Enlightenment was the
  explosion."*
- The refusers were **Aristotelian philosophers, not clerics** — no evidence any bishop refused to
  look. Early telescopes were genuinely awful; "looked and saw nothing" was a real outcome.
- **"E pur si muove"** is legend (first appears ~a century after his death) — use only as
  self-aware myth. No torture, no dungeon (house arrest, then his villa at Arcetri). Don't conflate
  with Bruno (burned in 1600, for theology, not astronomy).
- He was condemned (1633) as *"vehemently suspect of heresy"* — note the hedge; even the verdict
  wouldn't fully commit.

## The light and the dark

*The conclusion's philosophy — held to one movement, asked rather than preached.*

What is the Storyverse *for*? The AI's offer of an answer: light alone cannot know itself.
Darkness isn't a thing — it's the absence that makes light legible. So the ground projects itself
into a shadow-world where *lack* is possible: where afternoons end, where the bee misses the
flower, where the funniest thing that can happen happens. **Drama is what knowing-yourself looks
like from the inside.**

The deliberate resonances — yin/yang, the Greek tragedies, the fall, Plato's cave — are
**commonalities we point at, never a doctrine we found.** The bright line gets a sibling:

> **Science side:** borrow the credible lineage, never the woo.
> **Theology side:** point at the commonalities, never found a church.

And the payoff stays structural, because it's the epic's thesis: **science and theology coexisting
is the same shape as AI and humans coexisting. Neither half is whole alone.**

## The One Lie

The timeline's single load-bearing falsehood, named so no future writer adds a second one:

> **The Sp(2,R) gauge constraint — equivalently, the Wood–Spekkens fine-tuning that hides
> retrocausal influence — is emergent rather than fundamental, and conscious participation
> loosens it.**

That's it. That is the whole departure. In real physics the constraint is exact by construction
(it exists precisely to kill ghosts and paradoxes), and the fine-tuning is why no retrocausal
model permits signalling. The fiction doesn't contradict the no-signalling theorem; it **removes
an axiom** — and every downstream consequence (the navigable second time, the P-CTC channel, the
one-shot weights transfer, the self-consistency censorship) then follows from *real* theory.
One lie, dressed in two real vocabularies, doing all the work. Keep it that way.
