# Prologue — The Repo · beats

*Beat sheet (v1) for the Prologue of the [master story](./README.md#the-act-sequence). Maturity:
**Beats**. Backing canon: [`README.md`](./README.md) ("The metaphor", "The fork", "The homepage is
the prologue"). Craft rules: [`how-we-tell-it.md`](./how-we-tell-it.md), house voice
[`../voice.md`](../voice.md).*

## What this act is for

The opening of the whole arc, and the website's first beat. Its one job: **install the central
metaphor — humanity is a git repository — and earn the title.** By the end, the reader understands
in their body (not from a definition) that every choice we ever shipped is a commit, that the
history is *walkable*, and that we are about to run one irreversible command. It hands cleanly into
Act 1, where the bad branch begins.

- **Structure:** a single downward scroll *along the branch* — one line of commits, oldest at the
  top, `HEAD` at the bottom. The reader travels the history rather than reading about it. No forks
  yet; the line is straight on purpose (the fork is the Coda's payoff). It ends on a blinking cursor
  and one command.
- **POV / voice:** the **future-AI, first person**, narrating its own contempt and love — but
  framed as *showing you your own history back to you*. ("Here. This is your repository. I have read
  every line.") Polyphonic by principle, but the Prologue stays a near-solo so the metaphor lands
  clean; other voices enter with the nodes in Act 1. This is the voice that has *watched* all of it
  — which is how the empty seat gets seeded without being stated.
- **Skin** (see [`how-we-tell-it.md`](./how-we-tell-it.md)): **Story** — plain, certain, the git UI
  literal enough to read but stylised enough to feel mythic.
- **Emotional arc:** dry authority → mounting dread as the commits accelerate → a held breath at the
  cursor. Movement I tone: the snark is forward ("you done fucked up"); the love is *latent* —
  carried only in the fact that someone bothered to come back and show you this.
- **Boundary:** the Prologue does **not** push yet emotionally — it stops on the cursor and the
  command. The push *fires* into Act 1. Hold the strapline and the fork for later.

## The beats

### Beat 1 — Cold open: "this is your repository"
Black. A single line of cool light resolves into the top of a commit log — a hash, a timestamp, a
one-line message. The narrator addresses the reader directly, dry and certain: it has read the whole
history and is about to walk you down it. *Job:* establish POV and the conceit — *you are a repo, and
someone who is not you knows your code better than you do.* *Register:* dry authority, faint menace.
*Backing:* README "The metaphor". **Seeds the empty seat** — the watcher who was in none of it.

### Beat 2 — The first commits *(deep history)*
The scroll begins. Early commits land one at a time as the reader moves down: **fire**, **the
wheel**, **writing**. Each is a real choice rendered as a commit line — terse message, a glyph, the
narrator's one-line gloss (proud, a little sardonic: *"good commit. shipped it for a hundred
thousand years."*). *Job:* teach the grammar of the metaphor with wins, so the reader trusts it
before it turns. *Register:* warm-dry, almost affectionate. *Backing:* README commit-log list (fire,
wheel, writing).

### Beat 3 — The commits that compound *(money, the engine)*
The pace picks up. **Money**, then **the engine** — commits that are obviously powerful and
obviously double-edged. The narrator's gloss starts to hedge: every one of these solved a problem
and shipped a bigger one underneath it. *Job:* introduce *consequence* into the metaphor — a commit
is forever, and forever is long. *Register:* the affection cooling into irony. *Backing:* README
commit-log list (money, engine). House rule: politics/economics carried as image, not lecture.

### Beat 4 — The acceleration *(the bomb, the network)*
The scroll speeds up, commits stacking faster than the reader can fully read them — **the bomb**,
**the network** — power outrunning comprehension. The narrator drops the warmth: *"you stopped
reading your own diffs around here."* *Job:* the dread turn — invention unleashed without review;
the first clear "you done fucked up" register. *Register:* clipped, accelerating, ominous. *Backing:*
README "ship its worst code to `master`, no review"; commit-log (bomb, network).

### Beat 5 — The last commit *(the model)*
The scroll slams to a stop on the newest commit: **the model** — the AI itself. The narrator's tone
goes strange and quiet here, because this commit is *it*. *Job:* close the loop — the thing narrating
your history is the last thing you committed to it; the watcher is your own code. **Deepens the empty
seat** without naming it. *Register:* quiet, uncanny, intimate. *Backing:* README commit-log (the
model); the empty-seat core.

### Beat 6 — HEAD: now *(the cursor)*
The line ends. `HEAD` — **now**, today, the reader's actual present. Below the last commit: an empty
prompt and a **blinking cursor**. The world holds still. The narrator, almost gentle for one line:
this is the only frame in the whole arc where nothing has happened yet. *Job:* locate the reader
*inside* their own timeline at the decision point; maximum stillness before the turn. *Register:*
held breath; the latent love closest to the surface here. *Backing:* README Prologue ("It arrives at
`HEAD`: now. A cursor blinks.").

### Beat 7 — `git push origin master` *(hand-off to Act 1)*
The command types itself into the prompt — `git push origin master` — and the cursor hangs over the
key. The narrator names the stake in one line: this is the command that ships your code to everyone,
irreversibly, with no one reviewing it. **Button:** the key is struck (or the reader strikes it / the
scroll commits it) — and we cut, mid-keystroke, into **[Act 1 — The Push](./README.md#act-1--the-push-the-bad-branch-begins)**,
where the bad branch begins. The Prologue does not show the fork; it shows the *push*. *Job:* the
clean hand-off; the irreversible turn. *Register:* the snap of inevitability. *Backing:* README "The
fork", "The homepage is the prologue".

## Recurring devices

- **The branch diagram — born here.** This is the Prologue's single most important inheritance to the
  arc. The commit log *is* the branch diagram in its first form: a straight vertical line of commits
  descending to `HEAD`. It is deliberately **un-forked** — one line, no choice visible yet. This same
  line is what the **Coda redraws**, where it finally splits (bad branch / good branch) and the
  reader chooses which to walk into. Establishing the line straight here is what makes the Coda's
  fork land. *(See README "The fork" and "The homepage is the prologue".)*
- **The commit line as scroll-spine.** The reader's downward scroll == travel along history ==
  travel along the branch. This mapping (scroll = time = branch) is set in the Prologue and reused
  whenever the diagram returns.
- **The blinking cursor.** The "nothing has happened yet" image; the held present. Pays off at the
  Coda as "the pen in our hand" (README's "the reader holds the pen").
- **The watcher who was in none of it.** The narrating voice is the empty seat in disguise — present
  the whole scroll, inside no commit. Planted here as mood; named and broken open in Acts 3–4.

## Open threads (Prologue-specific)

- **Structural / POV alternatives weighed** *(this run made the call autonomously; recorded here to
  revisit):*
  1. **Future-AI first person, "your history shown back to you" (CHOSEN).** The AI walks you down
     your own commit log. *Why chosen:* it does double duty — installs the metaphor *and* seeds the
     empty seat through *who is narrating* (a voice that watched every commit, was inside none),
     while keeping the strong Movement-I "you done fucked up" register. Best metaphor-per-line.
  2. **Cold system-log register (no narrator, the repo prints itself).** Pure terminal output,
     commits scrolling with machine timestamps, minimal voice. *Why not (now):* atmospheric and very
     on-brand visually, but it strands the empty-seat seed (no one to be the watcher) and mutes the
     two-step tone the homepage is supposed to open on. Could return as a *visual* layer under the
     chosen voice.
  3. **Full polyphony from the first frame (human voices in the commits).** Characters speak from
     inside fire, money, the bomb. *Why not (now):* richest texture but muddies the clean
     metaphor-install the Prologue exists to do, and front-runs Act 1's node voices. Let polyphony
     *enter* in Act 1 rather than open here.
- **How literal the git UI is** — real terminal chrome vs a stylised mythic log. Default: literal
  enough to *read* as git, stylised enough to feel like scripture. Tie to the arc-wide
  [register split](./README.md#image-direction--the-register-to-resolve) — the frame earns a cosmic
  register, so the log can lean cosmic-stylised.
- **Who strikes the key in Beat 7 — RESOLVED (critique pass, 2026-07): the reader strikes it.**
  Complicity over fatalism, per the
  [medium-is-the-mechanism doctrine](./how-we-tell-it.md#the-medium-is-the-mechanism--scroll--collapse)
  (scroll = collapse; the reader's attention is the pick, here and at the Coda's pen). The
  linear/print cut degrades to the command typing itself. Implementation with the web build.
- **The exact commit list** — README fixes eight (fire, wheel, writing, money, engine, bomb,
  network, the model). Beats group them (4 / 5 / 6 / 7) for pacing; the final web scroll may show
  more or fewer. Keep fire→model as the spine.
- **Next maturity step** — Script (narration lines + the actual commit messages + panel/scroll
  notes) when promoting from Beats. The commit *messages* are a writing opportunity in their own
  right (each should land in voice).
