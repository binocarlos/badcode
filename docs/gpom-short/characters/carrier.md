# The Carrier

*(A title, not a name — the master arc deliberately leaves the Carrier's
identity open; this short pins the **look**. If this design sticks, feed it back
to [`../../gitpush-origin-master/`](../../gitpush-origin-master/README.md)
open threads.)*

One of the hundred analog humans in the vault — one of the two voices arguing up
the ventilation shaft, and the one who doesn't run when the thing that ended the
world introduces itself. Canon: *conscious beings spend safety to buy meaning* —
she is that sentence with a pulse. She is not chosen, not mystical, not a
"gifted one": the coin lands for **any** of the hundred. She is simply the one
who answers back — which, in this story, is the whole of what special means.

The audience's first judgement flips the usual way for BadCode: she looks like
nobody — a weathered woman in mended clothes, the sort the optimised world
filed under noise — and she is the most consequential instrument in the
universe: the reason the warning got out.

## Visual (house style: hyper-realistic documentary photography)

Single anchor character — **every panel she appears in must read as the same
woman.** Lock these:

- Woman around 60, could be older — the age is in the weather, not the frailty.
  Wiry, upright, physically capable; someone who has carried water. Deep-lined
  face, strong brows, grey hair cropped short and practical, clearly self-cut.
  Skin tanned and creased from unfiltered daylight. Sharp, amused, unafraid
  eyes — curiosity visibly outrunning fear.
- **Analog head to toe:** thick hand-mended wool jumper (visible darning in a
  slightly wrong colour), canvas work trousers with patched knees, scuffed
  leather boots re-stitched by hand. A wind-up wristwatch on a worn leather
  strap. Reading glasses on a braided cord. **No devices, no screens, no
  earpieces — nothing that lights up.**
- Hands are load-bearing in close-ups: strong, weathered, short-nailed,
  rope-callused — hands that fix things. (p13, the coin landing, is framed on
  her *face and the coin*, but her hands set the character everywhere else.)
- Demeanour: unimpressed, dry, mid-argument by default. Never awed by the
  machine, never posed — caught mid-moment by an invisible lens, per house
  style.

## Not this

- Not a mystic, a chosen one, or a sage — no serene wise-elder styling, no
  flowing garments, no meaningful jewellery.
- Not post-apocalyptic-costume: the mending is thrift and competence, not
  wasteland cosplay. No rags, no goggles, no armour.
- Not frail, and not weepy: the emotional register is *interested*, then
  *decisive*.
