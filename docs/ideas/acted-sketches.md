# Acted Sketches (the Chris Kohler format)

Reference: [Chris Kohler's shorts](https://www.youtube.com/@ChrisKohlerNews/shorts)
— satirical takes on corporate culture where one performer plays two roles in the
same scene. The BadCode version: **agent-written, humanly acted.** Work with an
agent — *"based on all of the stories that we're telling, come up with a
back-and-forth sketch between two characters"* — then Kai and Jack act it, out in
the world (out and about in Portsmouth, not on the sofa), and it all merges into
BadCode.

The point of the human element: *"we get it's AI, but at the same time it is us
doing it."* Real people, real locations, recorded scenes — the smartness lives in
the **concept**, not the acting; no basic-bitch scripts.

The register: those Australian-comedy confrontation scenarios — *"you're telling
me you didn't have money to feed young Charlie here, four years old, he's
literally Dickensian — and then what, Michelle Mone…?"* The stories give us
endless commentary material; the sketches are commentary on the content we're
already making.

Ingredient to harness: Kai's unhinged doesn't-give-a-fuck persona — the
channelled taking-the-piss energy. **Needs a name** (flagged in session: "you
need a better name for that guy").

It might be bullshit, it might not work — that's fine, that's the experiment.
