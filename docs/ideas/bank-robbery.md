# The Bank Robbery

A heist-genre satire about currency debasement, media distraction, and who's really
stealing from whom. Classic planning scene — dark garage, corkboard, red string, a crew you
root for: the Economist, the Spin Doctor, the Regulator, the Leader in shadow. The target:
"The biggest vault in the city. Everyone's life savings. Right there." The problem isn't
security — it's eyes. Too many people watching.

So the Spin Doctor unveils the distraction: "We don't need to be specific. Just say 'people
from over there' are coming. Taking things. Making it worse." — "From where?" — "Doesn't
matter. Everyone fills in their own 'them.'" The Regulator asks whether someone will notice
the money disappearing. The Leader, calm: **"It's not disappearing. It's just worth less.
So slow they'll blame each other before they blame us."**

The reveal: the camera pulls back and the garage was always a boardroom, the corkboard a
presentation screen, the robbers in suits — they aren't robbing the bank, they ARE the
bank. The genre betrays the audience the same way the system betrays citizens. The sharp
line, delivered to camera: **"The best robbery is one where the victims blame each other."**

Bad Code frames it from the future: "In my timeline, you never figured it out. You argued
about outsiders and insiders until there was nothing left to steal." Most sardonic closer:
*"Anyway. Enjoy arguing about immigration. I'm sure that'll fix your rent."*

The political point: while media runs on culture war, the real robbery is invisible —
money printing inflates assets, wages stagnate, wealth transfers upward, and the victims
blame each other.
