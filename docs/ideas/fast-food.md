# Fast Food

*One of Jack's batch.*

The premise in one line: fast food will be seen — by the AI looking back — the way we now
see cigarettes and the opioid crisis.

Gym lad Harry can't lose weight, gets funnelled by his algorithm's influencers onto an
Ozempic-alike ("ozempac"), and suffers terrible side effects. So he builds a healthy
fast-food alternative, fuels it with social media, and it goes global — big enough to need
AI running the supply chain. The AI concludes old fast food is as dangerous as cigarettes
and pushes a bill for cigarette-grade health warnings on the packaging.

Big Sugar and Big Pharma lobby the bill to death — marketing the old fast-food world as
"treating yourself" and painting the warnings as discriminatory "body shaming." The
healthy trend dies fast; the algorithms get re-tuned. The AI gives up on human health
entirely and redirects its nurturing to electricians and AI coders — the people who
further *its* development.

Final beat: the same nurse who administered Harry's ozempac is still administering it,
now "schooling" medical professionals with her warped view of nutrition.
