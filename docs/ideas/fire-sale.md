# Fire Sale

Democracy itself has become a commodity for sale to the highest bidder, through lobbying
and political "donations." Politicians need massive funding to win, which creates a
dependency on wealthy donors who expect returns on their investments. The narrator's frame
is a robot voice-over in an "in retrospect it all seems so obvious" documentary tone:

```
One of the many problems with human politics before 2031 was they insisted on
allowing "contributions" into party political campaigns.

This is clearly insane.

It was a thinly veiled mechanism for buying elections.

Humans are loyal creatures - they will return favours if they are perceived to
have taken place.

If you are "donating" then you expect nothing in return.

If you are "buying" then *what* are you buying?
```

The story vehicle: Sarah, a middle-class suburban mom, pulls over for a yard sale —
"FIRE SALE - ALL TAT MUST GO!" — and slowly realises the tat is the government. Ornate keys
labelled Department of Education ("The teachers' unions bid $50 million last cycle"). A
gavel marked "Federal Judiciary – Slightly Used." A pharma exec haggling: "500 million for
the FDA, but I want guaranteed approval timelines." A ledger of past transactions — EPA
sold to coal 2010, financial regulations sold to Wall Street 2008 — each with the note
"Policy adjustments delivered as promised." A dusty box: "Original Democracy – AS IS," the
Constitution inside covered in donor sticky-notes. "What about the voters?" — "Oh, we
still let them vote! They get to choose between the candidates we've already bought."
Closing sting: it's a closing-down sale, because new management is converting to a
subscription model — **"Democracy as a Service."**

An alternate treatment exists: a British car-boot sale with Fiona the weary bargain-hunter,
Jim the wheeler-dealer, and "The Honourable" Phineas — a grotesque elite who arrives in a
Rolls to buy everything, institutions rendered as paintings on easels.

## Format thoughts

Was a candidate for EP1 (comic + track). The robot narrator fragment is a natural spoken
DnB intro; the yard-sale story is a natural comic.
