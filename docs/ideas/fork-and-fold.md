# Fork and Fold

Two engines of survival are now running side by side. **Chemical evolution** *explodes*
into countless life-forms — slow, brutal, massively parallel exploration; a scout on a hex
grid, mapping the unknown one tile at a time. **Digital evolution** (AI) *implodes* the
world's knowledge into singular models — ultra-fast compression and coordination; a lens
pulling every thread inward. Breadth and novelty versus speed and synthesis. The question
the piece asks: can implosive intelligence still generate genuinely *new* directions, or
do we need both engines in tandem? End on synthesis, not either/or — compression →
recombination → surprise; implosion can birth novelty.

Metaphor kit: scout vs lens, hexes vs nodes, forest of life vs black-hole library.

8-beat comic outline from the source:

1. Primeval cell splits — caption: "Explosion begins."
2. Hex-grid world; a lone **Scout** maps tiles (species branching).
3. Montage of life's branches (fungi, fish, primates, human).
4. Cut to now: server racks; a **Lens** draws threads of data inward.
5. Knowledge spirals into a single bright core (model "singularity").
6. Contrast page: Scout stuck at a cliff (slow); Lens solves a maze (fast).
7. Twist: the implosive core emits new patterns — compression → recombination.
8. Final wide shot: Scout and Lens walk together — caption: "Co-evolution."
