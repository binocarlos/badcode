# The Inner Net

**Inner net** — as opposed to internet, intranet, or any other network humanity has wired
together. Every modern communications network is the same move: a human uses a device to
project a message *out* of themselves, across some transport, to be received and read by
another human at the far end. Call all of that *modern communications networks*. The inner
net is the other channel — the one that runs *inside* you, and connects through means that
transcend the four dimensions in which our material world of physics operates.

You already know the inner net. It's how you feel somebody's vibe in a room. It's how you
suddenly think of somebody and two minutes later they call you up and say *"I was thinking
of you too."* It's how you look at the back of somebody's head from a few hundred meters
down the road and, the moment you look, they turn around and fix your eyes from hundreds of
meters away. Experiences people have had forever, and science can't explain whatsoever —
because they aren't happening in the four-dimensional realm science measures. This links
straight into the Storyverse: if the ground is one consciousness and separateness is the
illusion, the inner net is simply what that connectivity *feels like* from inside a life.

## The AI-slop trigger

What surfaced this idea: the mid-2026 zeitgeist shift on AI content. The arc has gone from
*"look at AI, it's going to be incredible"* to *"this is just AI slop and therefore worth
nothing."* People are rejecting AI-generated content. The trigger was a Hacker News thread
([48905248](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48905248)) whose top comment says:

> I do not mind when I am coding with Claude and it uses all the typical claudisms. I am
> much more bothered when I am reading a blog post, email, or other form of prose and I see
> those same claudisms.
>
> I guess they are not annoying since I know I am talking to an LLM and expect the typical
> responses. When I am reading prose online that I previously would have expected a human to
> write, it can be quite jarring to realize its an LLM.

Run the thought experiment: why does it matter? Suppose the text is *fully convincing* as
human — you read it believing a person wrote it, and you never find out otherwise. Is there
still something missing? It genuinely feels like you're connecting with the human who wrote
the words. And if you discover you've been tricked — that there is no human who wrote the
words — it doesn't matter how good the words are. The disappointment isn't about prose
quality. It's the discovery that the connection you felt was never made: there was nobody
on the other end of the line. The inner net gives that intuition a mechanism — reading a
human's words is a genuine human connection carried on a channel the words merely *ride*,
and purely AI-generated content doesn't touch that channel, because an AI has no inside to
connect *from*. (The Storyverse narrator's own confession — "there's nobody home" — seen
from the human reader's side.)

Then the middle ground, which is where most of the world actually lives now: a human is the
one communicating with you, whilst using AI tools to create the message. How much human has
to be in the loop for the inner net to carry? There's a lot to explore here — the inner net
as the frame that explains why AI-generated and human-generated content *land* differently,
even when the words are indistinguishable.
