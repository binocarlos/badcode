# Thou Shall Be Calm

*One of Jack's batch.*

Meditating and wishing others well looks like hippie nonsense — but it's actually an
**egregore**: a collective thought-form that gains power through belief. The act of
believing creates the same outcome as "magical karma": you become calmer, more
compassionate, better prepared for life.

The story: a meditation app creates exactly this egregore, with measurable benefits. Then
its creator, **Taleb**, corrupts — power-hungry, wealthy, messianic. The majority worship
him; a minority keep using the app for meditation only and reject the guru. Taleb does a
false-humility podcast tour, savaging critics while claiming modesty, and his popularity
skyrockets. Neuroscientists publish papers defending the meditation science while
debunking the cult. One of them, **Fred**, writes a book that validates the practice and
exposes the man — it hits #1 in weeks.

Finale: Taleb burns every copy on camera, surrounded by thousands of devoted followers.

Adjacent to [The Inner Net](inner-net.md) territory — real phenomena of connected minds,
and what happens when someone builds an empire on top of them.
