# Karen

A middle manager at a big New York company, around her forties. We meet her being
exactly the stereotype the name carries — entitled, demanding, a complainer. The
story's job is to flip that judgement: her worst trait is the one society is about
to need most.

When the company's AI emails her into redundancy, she does the only thing she's
truly brilliant at — she complains. Egged on by her laid-off colleague, she takes
it to a phone box and starts climbing the management chain, refusing to be moved:
*"I've got all day to complain."* That persistence — annoying, immovable, total —
is the engine of everything that follows, from the viral hook to the President to
the revolution.

She is not redeemed by becoming nice. She is vindicated by being *exactly herself*
at the moment it counts. The point of her is: channel this. Up your Karen game.
