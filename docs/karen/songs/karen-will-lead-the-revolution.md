## karen will lead the revolution

```
I've got all day to complain
all day, all day to complain
```

**Key concept:**

> That Karen is a very positive force if we can focus their energy on affecting positive change. 

**Background:**

We will need some fundamental changes to the political system as we head into an era of full automation.

Private ownership of the automated means of the production will result in worse inequality than victorian times.

In order to really make people listen and be willing to change - we need to channel the energy of dissatisfaction into a positive force. The "Karen" stereotype - normally used derogatorily for persistent complainers - can be flipped into our greatest asset for political change. 

There is no better person equipped to not take "no" for an answer than Karen. What if we can mobilise an army of Karens to help us change the world?

**Story Scene 1:**

 * Karen loses her office job to AI and is very upset because she has put 30 years into the same company.
 * The way the company let her go was brutal - all she got was a £100 Amazon voucher.
 * She starts to look for another job and discovers that AI has replaced all the jobs she would be able to do.
 * This starts to really affect her, she can no longer afford birthday presents for her grandchildren.
 * Resigned to her fate, she starts to go through the process of applying for benefits.

**Story Scene 2:**

 * Karen rings up the benefits office and has a horrible experience speaking with an AI bot who does not understand her.
 * She spends a few weeks trying to get her benefits issue resolved and still fails.
 * Dejected - she speaks to her daughter who encourages her to persist calling up the benefits office.
 * Her daughter says: "Mum, you know how to get things done. Remember when you got that refund from the holiday company? Just keep calling."
 * Karen makes a plan to call up the benefits office every day until she gets through.

**Story Scene 3:**

 * Karen begins to phone the local benefits office every day. <!-- Persistence campaign begins -->
 * She is persistent and eventually gets through to someone who is not an AI bot.
 * She kicks off about how the system is broken and she needs her benefits NOW.
 * The local office says "I'm sorry, there's nothing more we can do at this level."
 * Karen's response is immediate: "That's not good enough. I want to speak to your manager."
 * She refuses to hang up until they escalate her case to the regional benefits office.

**Story Scene 4: The Great Escalation**

 * Karen works her way up the chain of command with unstoppable determination:
   * Regional benefits office: "This is unacceptable. I want to speak to YOUR manager."
   * Local MP's office: "You represent ME. I demand to speak to someone higher."
   * Civil servant in Whitehall: "The system is failing citizens. Who's YOUR boss?"
   * Home Secretary's office: "This is a national crisis. Put me through to the PM."
 * Each level tries to placate her, but Karen will not be deterred.
 * Finally, after weeks of relentless calling, she gets through to the Prime Minister.
 * "About bloody time," Karen says. "Now let me tell you what's happening to the middle class..."

**Story Scene 5: Karen and the PM**

 * The PM initially tries the standard political deflection: "We're doing everything we can..."
 * Karen interrupts: "No, you're NOT. I was an accounts manager for 30 years. Now I can't feed my grandchildren."
 * She explains how AI isn't just taking factory jobs anymore - it's decimating the middle class.
 * "When it was just the working class, you didn't care. But now it's US - the people who vote, who pay taxes, who ARE the economy."
 * The PM starts to realize this isn't going away: thousands of middle-class workers are losing everything.
 * Karen delivers the killer line: "Fix this, or I'll make sure every Karen in Britain knows exactly who to blame."
 * The PM asks what she wants. Karen says: "I want to speak to your manager."
 * "I... I don't have a manager. I'm the Prime Minister."
 * "Then YOU need to BE the manager and fix this. We're not going away."

**Story Scene 6: History Lesson (100 Years Later)**

 * A classroom in 2125. Children sit in a circle as their teacher begins a history lesson.
 * "Now class, who can tell me about the Great Karen Revolution of 2025?"
 * A student raises their hand: "That's when Karen Thompson refused to take no for an answer!"
 * The teacher nods: "Exactly. After her call with the PM, Karen didn't stop there."
 * The holographic display shows historical footage of Karen organizing:
   * She formed the "Managers Please Movement" <!-- Clever nod to the stereotype -->
   * Thousands of displaced middle-class workers joined her persistent calling campaigns
   * They overwhelmed every government department with polite but relentless demands
 * "The Karens," the teacher explains, "showed us that democracy requires active, persistent participation."
 * "They complained their way to universal basic income, worker protections against AI, and democratic ownership of automated systems."
 * A student asks: "So being a 'Karen' is a good thing?"
 * The teacher smiles: "It always was. They just needed the right cause to fight for."
 * The lesson ends with the class reciting the movement's motto: "We will not take no for an answer."
