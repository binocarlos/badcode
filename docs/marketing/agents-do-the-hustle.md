# Agents Do the Hustle

The division of labour, stated plainly in the 2026-07-22 evening session: **our
ideas are the weapon, not our hustle** — so the humans keep making stories, and
the agents do the promotion grind in the background. The canonical example given:
*"hey AI, pick a bit of this song, cut it, release it."* Every full-fat story
package (see [`short-form-funnel.md`](./short-form-funnel.md)) is a shelf of
material an agent can keep slicing and posting indefinitely, without new creative
work from us.

Explicitly acknowledged and explicitly deferred: somebody's got to do the hustle
or we're missing a trick — but tonight was not the night to "let the marketing
dollars into the room" (the Bill Hicks nod). Capture the shape, build it later.

## Automation shape

- **Trigger** — a story ships full-fat (comic / narrated video / music video /
  track), or a recurring tick while a story is in rotation.
- **Steps** — agent picks a slice (a song section, a comic sequence, a video
  cut), cuts it into a platform-shaped clip, drafts the caption in the BadCode
  voice.
- **Human gate** — drafts-for-approval. Nothing posts unattended unless that's
  decided on purpose later.

## Open questions

- Which platforms/accounts exist first (TikTok, Shorts, Reels, X…), and who owns
  the credentials?
- Cadence per story — how many slices a week before a story is "rinsed dry"?
- Which repo skills does the pipeline reuse (`animate-slide`,
  `music-video-short`, the assets pipeline)?
- Success signal — engagement per slice, traffic to the site, or follower growth?
- Relationship to the separate agent-framework project (the "BadCode government"
  agents) — this hustle-agent is plumbing, not one of the per-story characters;
  keep it in this repo's scope or over there?
