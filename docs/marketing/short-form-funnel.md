# The Short-Form Funnel

The distribution model settled in the 2026-07-22 Kai + Jack session, in one line:

> **short-form content → BadCode long-form art (the EP) → actual answers.**

Right now the priority is the masterpiece: get the EP1 body of work there
(Camping, Karen, Magic Money Tree, GitPush Origin Master — comic + tune + video
each). Then **everything short-form exists to funnel people to it** — and the EP
in turn funnels the hooked toward the deeper layer (Future Proof, the bad-code
government / Agent Orange work, which is deliberately deferred until the EP is
rolling).

## The levers

- **Cut the music videos into short-form clips** — TikTok / YouTube Shorts /
  Reels. The camping video already proves the material is worth an EP; the shorts
  are the trailer network for it.
- **The meme tier** — "fuck it, 10 seconds" content that stands alone but
  advertises the universe: [Kill Bots, Stand Down](../ideas/kill-bots-stand-down.md),
  [Manifesting BadCode](../ideas/manifesting-badcode.md).
- **The Future History series** — the evergreen engine:
  [the BadCode retelling of the next 50 years](../ideas/badcode-future-history.md)
  ("2047: this happened"), each clip a dispatch from the bad branch.

## Update 2026-07-22 (evening session) — stop blocking on the EP

The blocker named out loud: we were waiting to finish *everything* EP-related
before releasing anything. Decision: **don't. Put out the first one — Camping —
and rinse it.** Take small slices of it and turn them into small bits of content,
continuously, ruthlessly, **without doing new work**. The get-out-of-jail card:
once a piece of content exists, the marketing is just continuously taking
advantage of small aspects of it. "Really basic, really boring. We just do that."

**The full-fat story package.** A finished story isn't one artifact, it's a
whole shelf of cuttable material (some stories may ship semi-skimmed and miss an
element, but full fat is the target):

- the **comic** — the scrollable web page;
- a **narrated video of the comic** — because not everyone wants to scroll.
  Narration idea: rip off David Attenborough (the Asmongold channel uses an
  Attenborough narrator — that's the reference); nature-documentary authority
  over the panels;
- the **music video**;
- the **track** itself.

Marketing strategy = continuous release of small chunks of that package to
platforms. That's it.

**Posture:** *our ideas are the weapon, not our hustle.* Somebody's got to do
the hustle — that's acknowledged, a trick we'd otherwise miss — but the humans
keep making things while **agents do the hustle** in the background: see
[`agents-do-the-hustle.md`](./agents-do-the-hustle.md).

**Topicality:** news moments make the stories timely — e.g. Andy Burnham's just-
announced £380m homelessness campaign lands exactly on Camping's subject. Worth
watching for release-timing hooks. *(Figure as recalled in session — verify.)*
