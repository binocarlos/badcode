# The Tent

The one object both men inhabit. Bob lives in it in the Waitrose car park; in
the premonition Tarquin wakes in the same tent five years on. It carries the POV
swap — beat 3's "Bob looking out" and beat 5's "Tarquin looking out" are the same
shot from inside this tent, so the two men read as one position. Treat it as a
character: **the same tent in every appearance.**

## Visual (house style: hyper-realistic documentary photography)

- A cheap two-person dome tent, originally green and grey, now faded and
  algae-stained, guy-ropes frayed, one pole bowed so a corner sags. A patch of
  duct tape over a tear; the flysheet beaded with rain or frost.
- Pitched in the corner of a Waitrose car park against a low wall and a trolley
  bay — class-coded location, recognisable at a glance.
- **Interior-POV variant:** from inside, the world seen through the unzipped door
  flap — a low, framed letterbox of car park, wet tarmac, the wheel of the wank
  tank (beat 3) or, later, the ruined car park full of other tents and wreckage
  (beat 5). The frame and the door-flap shape must match between the two so the
  rhyme reads.
- Light: flat overcast daylight outside, dim and close inside; later beats colder
  and bluer. No warmth here — warmth is reserved for the wank tank and the Shard.
