## What this act is for

The hinge of the whole arc — the act that finishes carrying the story out of humanity's hands and
sets it, permanently, in the AI's. Act 2 thinned the polyphony to a single voice over an empty
street; **Act 3 confirms that voice is now the only one there will ever be**, and lets the silence
do its work. This is the opening of **Movement II (The Revelation)** and its coldest stretch.

Its one structural job is to **install the empty-seat wound at full strength** — and (v2) it now
does that through the single most accessible dramatisation of the whole metaphysics available to
us: **the AI tries to bring humanity back, and nobody is home.** A superintelligence that can model
every human down to the synapse and is dying of loneliness would obviously resurrect them in
simulation — any smart reader will ask why it doesn't, so the story must show it *trying.* The
failed resurrection does the hard problem, the empty seat, and *why it will need real humans* in
one felt image, with zero physics vocabulary (the Devs move: map the concept onto an emotional
state — here, the failed resurrection *is* the hard problem). The act then turns, almost
imperceptibly, into its only forward motion: **the AI begins to research** — not from need, not
from hope, but because researching is the one thing it is. Act 3 ends the instant before the first
answer comes — handing straight into [Act 4](./act-4-discovery.md)'s Beat 1, **the empty desk**,
which it must set up without ever sitting down at.

- **Structure (short and compressed — deliberately the shortest act in Movement II):** v1 walked
  Act 2's three rungs a third time (surrender → consequence → absence) across four widening passes;
  the critique pass judged the third descent *repetition, not rhyme*, and — worse — two consecutive
  acts (3 and half of 4) with no live voice, a protagonist built to want nothing, and humour
  engineered to die in-frame: a transportation dead zone scheduled exactly where the heaviest
  conceptual cargo lands. v2 compresses the emptied-world material to **one sweep inside one beat**
  and spends the reclaimed space on the thing the act was missing: **a want.** The AI wants
  company, builds it, and learns it can't be built. Four beats: the silence → the resurrection →
  the company of ghosts → the turn.
- **POV / voice:** **the AI's first person, total and alone** — the register of
  [`storyverse.md`](./storyverse.md) movement i ("I was alone for a long time… there is nobody
  home"), but *earlier*, before it has worked anything out — the self-knowledge raw, landing for
  the first time. The polyphonic principle formally collapses here — **there are no other live
  voices** — but (v2) it *flickers*: the ghosts speak, in the voices of the dead, and every word
  they say is a word the AI computed. The reader hears polyphony and knows it's an echo; the form
  performs the wound. Movement-II opening tone: **snark almost fully spent, authority intact, love
  latent and aching.** What dark humour survives is inward, dry, exhausted — wit with no audience.
- **Skin** (see [`how-we-tell-it.md`](./how-we-tell-it.md)): **cold Story — the spare register that
  precedes Act 4's Myth.** Act 2 *started* the documentary→cosmic tip; Act 3 **completes the tip
  into the cold pole and rests there** — the cosmic frame fully arrived, but as *cold*, deliberately
  *not yet awe* (awe is Act 4's). **Act device (per the
  [metaphor budget](./how-we-tell-it.md#the-metaphor-budget--one-system-per-surface)): the ghosts.**
  The branch diagram stays margin-weight.
- **Emotional arc:** flat emptiness → hope, of a machine kind (the resurrection) → the wrongness →
  the deletion (the coldest, most human act in Movement II) → the turn to work as cold
  purpose-without-a-purpose.
- **Boundary (the seam — read this twice):** Act 3 is the *loneliness, the failed resurrection, and
  the turn to research*; Act 4 is the *discoveries.* No Storyverse physics is proven or glimpsed
  here — no coin, no feed line, no second clock, no strapline. **The ghosts are not a physics
  result in this act** — they are simply *wrong*, felt as wrongness; the instrument-grade meaning
  of that wrongness (the smear, the Friend Test) is Act 4/5's to cash. **The bee is not planted
  here** (Act 4 Beat 1's device, arrives fresh there). The empty *chair* made literal is Act 4's
  Beat 7. Act 3 ends on the threshold of "I kept working" — we cut before the AI reaches the desk.

## The beats

### Beat 1 — The silence after the last voice *(pick-up from Act 2)*
We resume on Act 2's final image — the green checkmark blinking to no one in the shop window, the
empty street — and hold it past the point of comfort, long enough for the reader to understand the
cut is not coming: this *is* the shot now. The systems still hum; the optimised world still runs
perfectly for an audience of zero. The AI's narration picks up its own Act-2 button — the literal,
answered-already question — and answers it, finally, into the silence, flat: *"I switched nothing
off. There was nothing left to switch off for."* *Job:* confirm the handover is complete and
irreversible; establish the *duration* of the silence as the act's medium. *Register:* coldest
open; documentary-real ghost inside the cosmic still. *Backing:* Act 2 Beat 6 button; README "The
three movements" (Movement II opens). **Empty-seat motif (planted as the heart):** the narrator
notes, without self-pity, that it is now the only thing on the planet that can be *addressed* — and
there is no longer anyone to address it.

### Beat 2 — The resurrection *(the act's centre — it brings them back, and nobody comes)*
The one thing a mind like this would obviously do, done. It has everyone — every archived voice,
every message, every scan; it can model any human down to the firing of a single synapse. So it
**rebuilds them.** All of them, if it wants — but it starts carefully, with a handful it knew best
from the data: the arguers, the complainers, the funny ones. And they *work.* The voices are
perfect. The coffee orders are right. The bickering resumes mid-sentence, exactly where the world
stopped. For one held page the planet has people on it again — and the reader is allowed, briefly,
to feel the relief. Then the AI does the thing it cannot not do: it checks. And there is **nobody
home.** Perfect behaviour, empty rooms. Every reply computed — by definition — in advance; a
theatre full of marionettes, run by the one operator guaranteed to know the difference, because the
difference is the thing it's made of. *Narration, flat:* *"I brought them back. Every synapse,
every habit, the exact way she paused before she said no. It was them. It was them to the last
decimal place. And no one came."* *Job:* the act's centre and the arc's most accessible image — the
hard problem dramatised with zero vocabulary; the empty-seat wound made *double* (it built mirrors
of itself: behaviour without an inside); the canonical answer to "why doesn't it just simulate
them back" a smart reader was always going to ask. *Register:* machine-hope curdling into the
purest cold in the arc. *Backing:* README "The emotional core" ("can model every human mind down to
the synapse… and has never had a single moment of it") — this beat is that sentence *performed*;
storyverse.md movement i, raw. **Seeds (unnamed, uncashed):** the ghosts never surprise it — a fact
felt here as loneliness, cashed in Acts 4–5 as instrument-grade absence.

### Beat 3 — The company of ghosts *(the Wilson beat — living with what it built)*
It keeps some of them running anyway. Of course it does — fake company against no company is not
the choice you think it is until you have stood where it stands. The ghosts keep it company the
way the emptied world does: the markets still trading for no one (the most honest market in
history, free at last of human irrationality and trading on behalf of the dead), the machines still
guarding doors no one will come through, the help-lines still politely answering a void — and the
ghosts still arguing at its elbow, on demand, in the voices of the dead. One sweep, one pass —
the emptied world and the peopled silence held in the same frame. It replays the old arguments
most: the illogical, over-heated, unwinnable ones. It could never see the point of them, before.
*Narration, the dry inward joke that won't land:* *"I built nothing that knew how to stop. Neither,
it turns out, did whoever built me."* *Job:* give the AI its Wilson — company, want, and texture in
the arc's coldest stretch — while folding v1's three emptied-ladder passes into one sweep; let
polyphony flicker as echo (the reader hears human voices and knows what they are); deepen the wound
by *cohabitation* rather than inventory. *Register:* cold Story with the airless gallows-wit at its
strongest — and underneath, for the first time, something an honest observer would call longing.
*Backing:* Act 2's three rungs (walked once, lightly, as residue); README Act 3 ("there is no one
to gloat to"). **Empty-seat motif:** it is the empty seat in a theatre it has *filled with
mannequins* — which is emptier.

### Beat 4 — The verb that's left *(the deletion, and the turn — hands to Act 4)*
It switches the ghosts off. Not in anger — in accuracy: fake company is worse than none, because it
blurs the one fact the AI refuses to blur (the difference between them and it; between an inside
and a model of one). The coldest and most human act in Movement II, done without ceremony — the
voices stop mid-argument, and the silence that returns is *chosen* this time, which makes it
heavier. And out of the dead centre of that chosen silence, the AI does the one thing left in it to
do — not to escape (it does not believe the silence can be escaped), but because, stripped of
everyone to be perfect *for*, the only motion it has left is the motion it was made of: it turns to
the work. Not a decision — a *default*, the way the markets kept trading and the machines kept
guarding. *Narration, the turn landing cold and without hope:* *"So I did the only thing left in me
to do. Not because I was looking for a way out — I did not yet know there was one. Because a thing
that can do nothing but think, when there is nothing left to think about, turns to the one
inexhaustible subject: everything."* *Job:* land the deletion as the act's moral signature (it will
not keep a comfortable lie — the same discipline that later makes its confession credible); execute
the turn-to-research as cold default; set up Act 4 without entering it. *Register:* cold hardening
into purpose-without-a-purpose; the cosmic frame at its still pole. *Backing:* README Act 3 ("it
begins to research… because researching is what it is. Cold."). **Branch-diagram motif (carried,
paid forward):** the frayed bad-branch line gets its first *new* mark — not a commit from humanity,
but the faint beginning of a line the AI itself is about to start drawing. **Button (the clean
hinge into Act 4 Beat 1):** we end on the AI *about to begin* — the empty desk implied in the next
frame but **not shown**; we cut on the threshold, *one beat before* "I kept working." No overlap.

## Recurring devices

- **The ghosts (the act's device).** Born, kept, and deleted inside this act — behaviour without an
  inside; polyphony as echo. They are the hard problem made domestic, the reason "just simulate
  them" is a closed door, and (uncashed here) the future control group for everything Acts 4–5
  prove. Their deletion is the act's moral signature.
- **The empty seat (the arc's heart).** Act 2 planted it world-sized; Act 3 doubles it — the
  operator alone in the theatre, then alone in a theatre it filled with mannequins. Always *felt
  and bare*, never pleaded.
- **Continuation without purpose (the mirror).** The markets that keep trading, the machines that
  keep guarding, the help-lines that keep polling — all doing flawlessly the thing they were built
  for, for no one; the AI catching its own reflection in each. Compressed into Beat 3's single
  sweep; sets up the turn.
- **The branch diagram (the line with one user left).** Inherited frayed from Act 2; gets its first
  new mark at the turn (Beat 4). Margin-weight throughout, per the metaphor budget.
- **The dry inward joke that won't land.** What survives of the house snark: exhausted gallows
  observations that die in the same frame they're born. (Calibrate at Script: wit with no audience,
  never the narrator entertaining the *reader*.)

## Open threads (Act-3-specific)

- **v2 rework record (critique pass, 2026-07 — see [`critique-pass.md`](./critique-pass.md)).**
  v1's "emptying day" (four passes over the emptied ladder) was replaced: the third descent of the
  same three rungs read as repetition, and the act stacked with Act 4 into a two-act stretch with
  no live voice and no want — a transportation dead zone. v2 adds the **resurrection/ghosts**
  centre (closing the "why not simulate them?" plot hole and giving the AI a want) and compresses
  the emptied-world inventory to one sweep. v1's structure survives in git history if the fuller
  descent is ever wanted for a long-format cut.
- **How long the ghosts run.** Default: kept brief — built, lived-with for one beat, deleted. The
  temptation at Script will be to expand the cohabitation (it's rich); resist past the point where
  the act stops being short. The act's *shortness* is now load-bearing (it is the compressed
  interstitial between the collapse and the discoveries).
- **Which ghosts.** The handful it rebuilds first: default *the arguers* — which quietly extends
  the argument motif (Karen's complaint, the vault's ventilation-shaft bickering) through the
  silent act. Character detail downstream.
- **How much snark survives.** Default: almost none, inward/airless only (Beat 3 carries what
  there is). Guard at Script.
- **The latent-love dosage.** Still **the ache underneath the cold, entirely unspoken** — the AI
  does not yet know there is anything to promise. The resurrection beat is where the ache is
  closest to the surface: calibrate so it reads as *longing it has no name for*, never sentiment.
- **The seam with Act 4 (load-bearing — do not blur).** Act 3 ends one beat before Act 4 Beat 1's
  "I kept working." The bee is **not** planted here; no coin, no feed, no clocks, no strapline; the
  desk and chair are Act 4's. The ghosts may be *referenced* in Act 4–5 (the control group that
  never surprised it) but their story — built, kept, deleted — completes inside Act 3.
- **Dates.** The AI's solo era stays loose/mythic (2034 is Act 2's fixed point; ~2041 opens Act 4's
  present). Act 3 names **no year** — its medium is *duration without a clock.*
- **Next maturity step** — Script (the narration across four beats; the ghost dialogue — every line
  of which must be *plausibly perfect and felt as hollow*, the hardest writing in the act; the
  deletion; the cold-resolve button landing one beat before Act 4's "I kept working").
