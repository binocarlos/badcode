# EP1 — the teaser

**EP1** is exactly that: a short teaser — the first thing the world sees from BadCode.
The name is deliberately plain (`EP1`, no clever title), because the point isn't to overclaim, it's
to *hook*. EP1 sits beneath the overarching project, **[GitPush Origin Master](./README.md)** —
the large timeline everything else hangs from.

> **Revised 2026-07-22 (Kai + Jack session):** EP1 is now **four stories** —
> **Camping, Karen Will Lead the Revolution, Magic Money Tree, and GitPush Origin Master**
> (the title track, which ends by manifesting BadCode itself). **Emperor's New Coin moves to the
> in-development pool** — its software/interactive ambitions (the bad-code government, Agent
> Orange) are deliberately deferred until the EP is rolling.

How we frame it publicly: **BadCode — EP1, from _GitPush Origin Master_ (coming soon).**

Each EP1 story ships as **two** things for now:

1. **A comic** — AI-generated imagery, presented as a scroll-driven story on this site.
2. **A music track** — drum & bass.

> The per-story **AI agent** (a topic specialist in the fictional "BadCode government") is **not**
> part of EP1. It comes later and is built in a separate project. We launch with comic + track only.

## Tracklist

Each story's full canon (concept, characters, beats, device) lives in its own
`docs/stories/<story>/` folder — the source of truth, linked per entry. These blurbs are
summaries derived from it.

### 1. Camping
Wealth inequality and the coming automation of white-collar work. Tarquin — a
middle-aged City financier, all spreadsheets and fast cars — sneers at Bob, a
homeless man living in a tent in the Waitrose car park. That night, off his face
at an executive ayahuasca retreat in Wales, Tarquin sees his reflection in a
river and *blinks* (a scroll-driven effect) into an old, bedraggled version of
himself — then blinks again, five years on, waking inside Bob's tent in a car
park full of tents after the AI has taken even the C-suite. Bob, who has every
right to gloat, shows him grace instead. The point: the people doing everything
"right" are the least prepared for what's coming.
→ Canon: [`../camping-v2/`](../camping-v2/README.md). *(Ending decided — Bob's grace, bleak-but-friendly
car-park makeup; status 2026-07-22: effectively done, a few slide edits left.)*

### 2. Karen Will Lead the Revolution
The "Karen" stereotype flipped into an asset: persistence. A New York middle
manager, made redundant by the company's AI, does the one thing she's brilliant
at — complain. From a phone box she escalates "I want to speak to your manager"
up the whole chain, holding station for days ("I've got all day to complain")
until a roaming street producer, Ari at Home, turns her rant into a hook that
goes viral. The meme carries her onto a late-night show, patched live to the
President — the "ultimate manager" — just as his red phone lights up: the AI has
locked the cabinet out and made *them* redundant too. The buck never stopped with
him. He calls Karen back to work together, and the revolution begins. A century
on, a lecture hall of humans and androids studies the Karen-led Revolution of
2026; a bronze statue of her sits in the phone box. Nobody is better equipped to
refuse "no" than Karen.
→ Canon: [`../karen/`](../karen/README.md).

### 3. Magic Money Tree
The blatant-lie moment of the whole economic story. BBC Question Time, 2017: a
nurse asks about her pay and Theresa May tells her *"there is no magic money
tree."* The set literally **crumbles** — because the line isn't true — into 2020
Downing Street: COVID, Boris and Rishi needing £300bn out of nowhere (VIP PPE
lane humming). They walk into the Bank of England, open an inner vault, and
there it is: the tree that makes the paper the money is printed on, feeding the
money printer. There *is* a magic money tree — it's called the money printer,
and there's no magic to it. Kept deliberately short; final Easter-egg image:
Michelle Mone's boat in Monaco.
→ Canon: [`../magic-money-tree/`](../magic-money-tree/README.md).

### 4. GitPush Origin Master (title track)
The umbrella story told as its own release: the reader rides inside an
unpersonified AI watching humanity undo itself, gets the boredom, the Storyverse,
the vault, the experiments, the adjacent particle — and the revert. The ending
is the arrival: *"and here we are. We are BadCode."* The ultimate introduction
to who's been talking the whole time. The hook already exists and lands with
people who've never seen the thing: *"git push origin master / this code is a
fucking disaster / the developers ain't fixing shit / and the compiler's stuck
on 16-bit"* — the track can carry itself on the hook, upgraded verse over verse,
over dystopian sounds.
→ Canon: [`./README.md`](./README.md) (the backbone).
