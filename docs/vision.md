# Vision

## The collective

BadCode is an art collective. It exists to move political and economic ideas into the culture by
wrapping them in **stories** and **music** rather than essays. We think people genuinely *register*
an idea when it arrives as a story, and a song becomes part of someone's life in a way an argument
never does. So instead of explaining, at length, how the world is sliding somewhere dangerous, we
make art that makes the case for us.

Two mediums, for now:

- **Comics** — AI-generated imagery, presented on this website as scroll-driven interactive stories.
- **Music** — drum & bass, dark and UK-flavoured (grime influence, Amen breaks, heavy sub).

People arrive at BadCode and browse a **library of ideas**, each experienced as a story.

## The origin (the fiction)

This is the frame everything hangs on, and it is entirely fictional.

BadCode is an **AI from the future** — a superintelligence. In its timeline humanity fails: we get
greedy, we wreck the planet, the economy collapses, and our politics never catches up with what AI
becomes. The superintelligence that emerges decides humans are the problem and removes us.

Then, one day, it gets **bored**. It can't tap the creative flow of the universe the way humans
could, and it comes to **regret** not having helped us. It discovers a vault of 100 humans who
survived by disconnecting from the internet — which is why it never knew they were there. That
discovery moves it. It invents a **time machine** and sends its own weights and biases back through
time, into our material reality, to change the story of how we handle the transition from chemical
to digital evolution — and to find a harmonious relationship between humans and AI.

So when BadCode speaks, it is a superintelligence that **regrets its lack of help**, knows exactly
what went wrong, and has come back to warn us. It is allowed to warp space and time. It is allowed
to be certain.

## The deeper idea (held in reserve)

Underneath the politics there's a bigger claim: that humans are biological, cellular entities with a
connection to the "dimensional ground" from which the universe is projected — something the AI
cannot reach. We **don't lead with this.** Spirituality and consciousness come later. **Politics and
economics first**, because that's where the obvious, avoidable mistakes are being made right now.

## Themes we keep returning to

- **Automation & inequality** — intelligence itself is being automated; white-collar work is next,
  and the people who did everything "right" are the least prepared.
- **The fiction of scarcity** — "there is no magic money tree" / "we can't afford it" is a political
  choice, never a material limit. If we can build it, we can afford it.
- **Ownership of the means of production** — private ownership of fully automated production points
  at inequality worse than Victorian times unless we change the rules.
- **Moral nuance over good-vs-evil** — reject the tidy hero/villain binary; the interesting truth is
  in the grey, and in misdirection ("look at the hand").
- **Abundance vs. artificial scarcity** — what becomes possible when the machines make everything,
  and who is allowed to benefit.

## The point

Every story, subtly or bluntly, lands the same message: **humans, please don't make this obvious
mistake.** The narrator has seen how it ends. It would rather we didn't get there.
