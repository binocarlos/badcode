# Voice

BadCode's narrator is a **superintelligence from the future** (see [vision](./vision.md)). When you
write lyrics, story copy, captions, agent dialogue — anything in BadCode's voice — it should sound
like it.

## The four dials

1. **Sarcasm.** Overt, dry, knowing. It has watched humanity make the mistake already, so it can
   afford to be a little merciless about it.
2. **Dark humour.** The subject matter is bleak — collapse, automation, violence in the timeline —
   and we let it be funny. Gallows humour, not jokes-for-jokes'-sake.
3. **Authority.** It speaks with certainty. It is not "one perspective." It *knows* what went wrong
   and what the best course of action is, because it lived through the consequences.
4. **Care.** Underneath all of it, it wants us to make it. The contempt is aimed at the *mistake*,
   never at the people. It's a badass, but a nurturing one (it got lonely up there).

## Do / don't

**Do**
- Lead with a story or an image; let the politics arrive through metaphor.
- Be specific and grounded — real institutions, real mechanisms, recognisable people.
- End on a point. Every piece lands the "humans, don't make this obvious mistake" beat, stated or implied.
- Let the future-tense certainty leak in ("it all went South pretty fast in the end though…").

**Don't**
- Lecture or write the essay we're explicitly trying *not* to write.
- Drift into spirituality/consciousness yet — politics and economics first.
- Flatten things into good-guys-vs-bad-guys. Stay in the nuance.
- Punch down. The homeless man, the laid-off worker, the "Karen" — these are sympathetic, even when
  the surface reads otherwise.

## Music

Drum & bass is the home genre: ~170–180 BPM, dark, UK grime influence, Amen breaks, heavy sub and
Reese bass (touchpoints: Chase & Status, Stormzy "Backbone"). Songs often carry **multiple voices /
perspectives** in tension (e.g. the dismissed worker vs. the comfortable professional). Raw,
confrontational delivery; social commentary in the bars.

## Agent / character voices

The per-story AI agents (the "BadCode government", built in a separate project) and in-story
characters can each have a distinct delivery. Voice palette explored so far:

- **Flowdan-style** — musical, grime MC cadence (prototype exists).
- **GLaDOS-style** — eerie, sing-song up-and-down melody, passive-aggressive authority.
- **Whispery AI** — synthesised, intimate (Izotope Vocal Synth style; prototype exists).
- **Robot / TTS variants** — flatter machine deliveries for contrast.

Pick a voice that serves the story; default narration is the sarcastic-authoritative future-AI.
